# Chapter 3 — Market Structure

[← Candlestick Mastery](02-candlestick-mastery.md) | [Volume 3 Home](README.md) | [Next: Support & Resistance →](04-support-resistance.md)

---

If candlesticks are the alphabet, **market structure is the grammar** — the skeleton that organizes every chart. Read structure correctly and you'll know the trend, where it might turn, and whether any given setup is *with* or *against* the dominant flow. This is arguably the **single most important technical skill**, and most beginners skip it to chase patterns. Don't.

---

## 3.1 The building blocks: swing highs and swing lows

Price doesn't move in straight lines; it moves in **swings** — pushes and pullbacks. The turning points are:

- **Swing high:** a peak — a candle high with lower highs on both sides. A local top.
- **Swing low:** a trough — a candle low with higher lows on both sides. A local bottom.

```
        SH                         SH = swing high
       /  \        SH              SL = swing low
      /    \      /  \
   SL       \    /    \
             \  /      SL
              SL
```

Connecting these swing points reveals the structure. **Everything else in this chapter is built from swing highs and swing lows.**

---

## 3.2 The three states of the market

Every chart, on every timeframe, is in one of three states. Identifying which is your **first job** on any chart.

### Uptrend — Higher Highs (HH) and Higher Lows (HL)
Each swing high exceeds the last; each pullback bottoms higher than the last. Buyers in control.

```
            HH
           /  \      HH (new high)
     HH   /    \    /
    /  \ /      \  /
   /    HL       HL   ← each low is HIGHER than the previous
  HL
```

### Downtrend — Lower Highs (LH) and Lower Lows (LL)
Each swing high is lower; each push down bottoms lower. Sellers in control.

### Range — sideways
Swing highs cluster around the same ceiling; swing lows around the same floor. No directional control; price oscillates. (Markets spend a *lot* of time ranging.)

> 🔑 **"The trend is your friend."** Trading **with** the structure (longs in an uptrend, shorts in a downtrend) is materially higher-probability than fighting it. Counter-trend trading is an advanced skill — default to with-trend.

---

## 3.3 Reading the trend, step by step

```
1. Pick your timeframe and zoom out enough to see several swings.
2. Mark the obvious swing highs and swing lows.
3. Ask: are highs rising and lows rising? → UPTREND (look for longs).
   Are highs falling and lows falling?    → DOWNTREND (look for shorts).
   Are highs & lows flat/overlapping?      → RANGE (trade the edges or stand aside).
4. Locate price NOW: early in the trend, extended, or pulling back?
```

The **best with-trend entries come on pullbacks** to a higher low (uptrend) or lower high (downtrend) — you join the trend at a discount, with a tight stop just beyond that swing point.

---

## 3.4 Break of Structure (BOS) — trend continuation

A **Break of Structure** confirms the trend is *continuing*: price breaks **beyond the most recent swing** in the trend's direction.

- In an uptrend, price closing **above the prior swing high** = bullish BOS → uptrend intact/resuming.
- In a downtrend, price closing **below the prior swing low** = bearish BOS → downtrend intact/resuming.

A BOS is your confirmation that momentum still belongs to the trend. Many traders enter on the **pullback that follows a BOS**.

---

## 3.5 Change of Character (CHoCH) — the first sign of a turn

A **Change of Character** is the *first* structural hint that a trend may be **reversing**: price breaks a swing point **against** the prevailing trend.

- In an **uptrend** (HH/HL), the first time price breaks **below a higher low** → bullish structure broken → potential shift to downtrend.
- In a **downtrend** (LH/LL), the first time price breaks **above a lower high** → potential shift to uptrend.

```
Uptrend → CHoCH → possible downtrend:

        HH
       /  \
   HL /    \         ← price fails to make a new HH...
  /        \
 /          \____   ← ...and breaks BELOW the last Higher Low = CHoCH
                 \     (character has changed; trend may be turning)
```

> 🔑 **BOS = "the trend continues." CHoCH = "the trend may be ending."** Together they let you describe, in real time, whether a market is trending, pausing, or turning — without a single indicator.

---

## 3.6 Pullbacks vs reversals — the eternal question

Is this dip a **pullback** (buy the dip, trend continues) or the start of a **reversal** (get out / flip)? Structure gives you objective criteria:

| | Pullback (continuation) | Reversal |
|---|--------------------------|----------|
| **Structure** | Holds the last HL (uptrend) | Breaks the last HL → CHoCH |
| **Depth** | Shallow–moderate (often to a level/Fib) | Deep; reclaims prior ranges |
| **Momentum** | Weak counter-candles, drying up | Strong impulsive counter-candles |
| **Action** | Look to join the trend | Stand aside or trade the new structure |

The honest answer: **you never know for sure in advance** — which is exactly why you use a **stop** beyond the structural level. If it was a reversal, you're out small; if a pullback, you're positioned well. Structure tells you *where* that line is.

---

## 3.7 Structure across timeframes

Structure is **fractal** — it exists on every timeframe, and they can disagree:
- The **daily** can be in an uptrend while the **15-minute** is in a downtrend (a pullback on the daily *is* a downtrend on the 15-min).
- This isn't a contradiction — it's the key to timing. You use the **higher timeframe for bias** and wait for the **lower timeframe structure to align** with it (a lower-timeframe CHoCH back in the HTF direction is a classic entry trigger). Full method in Chapter 8.

---

## 3.8 Liquidity, stop-hunts, and false breaks

Markets often **spike beyond an obvious swing high/low and then reverse.** Why? Clusters of stop-loss orders sit just beyond obvious swing points; price is drawn to that "liquidity," triggers the stops, then moves the other way.

- A break that **closes** beyond a level and *holds* = likely genuine (a real BOS).
- A wick that pokes beyond and **closes back inside** = likely a **false break / liquidity grab** — often a high-probability reversal *with* the prior structure.
- **Practical defense:** place stops with a **buffer beyond** structure (not exactly on the obvious level), and prefer **candle closes** over intrabar pokes for confirmation.

> 🔑 Don't put your stop where everyone else's is — one pip under the obvious low. Give it room beyond the structure so a routine liquidity grab doesn't take you out of a correct trade.

---

## 3.9 Chapter summary

```
• Structure is built from SWING HIGHS and SWING LOWS.
• 3 states: UPTREND (HH/HL), DOWNTREND (LH/LL), RANGE (flat). Identify this FIRST.
• Trade WITH structure; pullbacks to HL/LH are the prime with-trend entries.
• BOS = trend continues (break in trend direction).
• CHoCH = trend may be turning (first break against the trend).
• Pullback vs reversal is judged by whether structure holds — and a STOP covers the unknown.
• Structure is fractal: HTF = bias, LTF = timing.
• Beware false breaks/liquidity grabs; favor closes and place stops with a buffer.
```
