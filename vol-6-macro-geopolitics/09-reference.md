## A. Economic indicator cheat sheet (Ch. 3)

> Effect shown is the *typical* reaction in a normal/inflation-fighting regime. **Always check the regime first (Ch. 5)** — the same data can flip meaning.

| Indicator | Tier | Frequency | What a BEAT (vs forecast) usually means |
|-----------|------|-----------|------------------------------------------|
| **Central-bank rate decision & guidance** | 1 | ~6 weeks | Hawkish surprise → **currency UP** |
| **CPI / Core CPI (inflation)** | 1 | Monthly | Hotter → hike bets → **UP** (until "out of control") |
| **PCE (Fed's preferred inflation)** | 1 | Monthly | Hotter → hawkish Fed → **USD UP** |
| **Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP)** | 1 | Monthly (1st Fri) | Stronger → **USD UP** (violent spikes) |
| **Average hourly earnings (wages)** | 1 | With NFP | Hotter → inflation → hawkish → **UP** |
| **GDP** | 1 | Quarterly | Stronger growth → **UP** |
| **PMIs / ISM (mfg & services)** | 1 | Monthly | >50 & beat → **UP**; <50 → contraction → **DOWN** |
| **Retail sales** | 2 | Monthly | Stronger consumer → **UP** |
| **Jobless claims** | 2 | Weekly | Fewer claims → **UP** (high-freq labor read) |
| **PPI (producer inflation)** | 2 | Monthly | Hotter → leading inflation signal → **UP** |
| **Consumer/business confidence** | 2 | Monthly | More optimism → **UP** (leading) |
| **Trade balance / current account** | 2–3 | Monthly | Surplus → structural support |
| **Durable goods / industrial production** | 2–3 | Monthly | Stronger → mildly **UP** |

**Leading** (predict): PMIs, building permits, consumer expectations, yield curve, stocks.
**Coincident** (now): GDP, employment, industrial production. **Lagging**: unemployment rate, CPI (somewhat).

## B. Central-bank quick guide (Ch. 2)

| Bank | Currency | Watch for |
|------|----------|-----------|
| **Fed (FOMC)** 🇺🇸 | USD | Center of gravity; dot plot; dual mandate; "Fed vs the rest" drives major trends |
| **ECB** 🇪🇺 | EUR | Eurozone-wide policy; price-stability mandate |
| **BoE (MPC)** 🇬🇧 | GBP | Published vote splits signal the path |
| **BoJ** 🇯🇵 | JPY | Long ultra-loose; JPY = haven & carry funder; shifts are huge |
| **SNB** 🇨🇭 | CHF | Haven; history of intervention (2015 floor removal) |
| **BoC** 🇨🇦 | CAD | Oil-sensitive currency |
| **RBA** 🇦🇺 | AUD | Commodities & China proxy |
| **RBNZ** 🇳🇿 | NZD | High-beta, risk/commodity-sensitive |

```
Decode every decision: HAWKISH (tighten) = currency-positive | DOVISH (ease) = negative.
Trade the SHIFT vs what's priced in. Watch REAL rates (nominal − inflation) and the
balance sheet (QE = liquidity up/weak currency; QT = liquidity down/supportive).
```

## C. Risk-sentiment & safe-haven map (Ch. 5)

```
RISK-ON (greed)  → money to high-yield/growth: AUD, NZD, CAD, EM (havens & sometimes USD fall)
RISK-OFF (fear)  → money to SAFE HAVENS: USD > JPY / CHF > gold   (risk FX sold)

Read the regime via: stock indices, the VIX, bond yields, haven FX, credit spreads.

USD SMILE: USD strong in (1) global panic [haven] and (2) US outperformance [yield];
           weak when the US muddles while the world grows.
```

## D. Intermarket relationships (Ch. 6)

```
BONDS/YIELDS  → closest FX link. Rising relative yields → currency UP. Yield DIFFERENTIALS
                track major pairs (watch the 2-yr). Inverted curve = recession warning.
COMMODITIES   → CAD↔oil | AUD↔metals/China | NZD↔soft commodities | NOK↔energy.
                Oil up: helps exporters, hurts importers, feeds inflation.
GOLD          → often inverse to USD/real yields; haven & real-yield barometer.
STOCKS/VIX    → risk-sentiment gauge. Risk-on: risk FX & USD/JPY up. Risk-off: havens up.
⚠️ Correlations SHIFT with the regime — use as context/confluence, not fixed rules.
```

## G. The research routine (Ch. 7)

```
DAILY (20–40m):  calendar + overnight news + risk regime → update scorecards → plan events
WEEKLY (1–2h):   review scorecards & theses → map the week's events → rank pairs by conviction
PER EVENT:       know forecast (priced in) → note actual vs forecast AND the reaction
MONTHLY:         dominant themes? cleanest strong-vs-weak pairing? review macro-call performance
SOURCES:         economic calendar, central-bank primary docs, yields/commods/VIX, COT.
                 Pressure-test others' views; ignore guru noise; track the CONSENSUS.
FOCUS:           go DEEP on 2–4 currencies / a few pairs. Depth > breadth.
```

## H. The full decision method (Ch. 8) — pin this up

```
MACRO BIAS (scorecard/thesis)        → which way to trade the pair
   → RISK REGIME check (risk-on/off)  → does the regime support it?
   → INTERMARKET confluence            → yields/commodities/stocks agree?
   → TECHNICAL timing (Vol 3)          → quality level + trigger in the bias direction
   → EVENT check (calendar)            → high-impact release imminent? plan around it
   → RISK & SIZE (Vols 1, 4)           → stop at invalidation, ≤1–2%, reward ≥2× risk
   → MANAGE (Vol 4)                    → break-even, trail, scale; hold themes w/ conviction
   → JOURNAL (Vol 4)                   → record macro reasoning; measure if it adds edge

CORE TRUTHS:
  • Trade the RELATIVE, expectations-adjusted outlook — not the raw headline.
  • The SURPRISE moves price; respect what's already priced in.
  • Identify the RISK REGIME first; it decides how all data is interpreted.
  • Macro = bias & conviction; technicals = timing; risk = survival. Fuse all three.
  • Think in THEMES, go DEEP on a few markets, hold views flexibly, be patient.
```
