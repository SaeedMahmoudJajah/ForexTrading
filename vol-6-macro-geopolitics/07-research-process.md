# Chapter 7 — Building Your Macro Research Process

[← Intermarket Analysis](06-intermarket.md) | [Volume 6 Home](README.md) | [Next: Trading the Fundamentals →](08-trading-fundamentals.md)

---

Knowledge of indicators is useless without a **process** to turn it into an up-to-date, actionable view. This chapter is the practical engine of the volume: exactly *how to study the market* — the sources, the routine, and the framework — so that at any moment you have a clear, defensible **macro bias** for every currency you trade. This is the daily work that separates the top 1% from people who "watch the news."

> 🔑 **The goal of macro research is one deliverable: a clear, current directional BIAS for each currency, with the reasoning behind it.** Everything below serves that. If your research doesn't end in *"I'm fundamentally bullish/bearish on X versus Y, because…,"* it's just consuming news.

---

## 7.1 The currency scorecard — your core framework

For each currency you trade, maintain a simple **scorecard** that rolls the whole volume into one verdict. Score each factor as bullish (+), neutral (0), or bearish (−) for that currency:

```
CURRENCY: __________                         (vs its pair partner)
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────┬──────────┐
│ FACTOR                                          │  +/0/−   │
├────────────────────────────────────────────────┼──────────┤
│ Monetary policy path (hawkish/dovish vs priced) │          │  ← biggest weight (Ch. 2)
│ Rate differential & direction (carry)           │          │
│ Inflation trend (vs target / vs expected)       │          │  (Ch. 3)
│ Growth & PMIs (momentum, leading)               │          │
│ Employment & wages                              │          │
│ Risk sentiment fit (haven? risk currency?)      │          │  (Ch. 5)
│ Intermarket (yields, commodities, stocks)       │          │  (Ch. 6)
│ Geopolitics / specific risks                    │          │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────┴──────────┘
NET BIAS for the currency: ____________   Conviction: low / med / high
```

**Then compare two currencies' scorecards** to form a pair bias:
```
EUR scorecard NET: bearish    USD scorecard NET: bullish    →  EUR/USD bias: BEARISH (short)
```

> 🔑 **A currency's strength is only meaningful relative to its pair partner.** The strongest trades pair a **fundamentally strong** currency against a **fundamentally weak** one (e.g. long the hawkish, hard-data-beating currency vs the dovish, slowing one). Scoring both sides is the whole point.

---

## 7.2 Build a macro thesis per currency/pair

Turn the scorecard into a written **thesis** — a one-paragraph story you can trade from and hold through noise (Vol 2's "themes"):

```
THESIS TEMPLATE (one per pair you actively trade):
  PAIR: ______   BIAS: long / short   CONVICTION: ___
  THE STORY (1–3 sentences):
    "The Fed is [hawkish/dovish] and [ahead of/behind] inflation, while the ECB is ___.
     Rate differentials are [widening/narrowing] in ___'s favor. The risk regime is
     [risk-on/off], which [supports/pressures] this pair. Therefore I expect ___ over
     [weeks/months]."
  KEY DRIVERS I'M WATCHING: __________________________
  WHAT WOULD INVALIDATE THIS THESIS: ________________   ← define it now (flexibility, Ch.1)
  KEY UPCOMING EVENTS (calendar): ___________________
```

A written thesis gives you **conviction to hold winners** through noise *and* a pre-defined trigger to **flip when the story genuinely changes** — the exact balance the macro legends strike (Vol 2).

## 7.3 The research routine — how to actually study the market

Consistency beats intensity. A repeatable rhythm keeps your scorecards and theses current without drowning you in noise.

```
── DAILY (20–40 min) ─────────────────────────────────────────
  □ Scan the ECONOMIC CALENDAR: today's high-impact events & forecasts (Ch. 4)
  □ Check overnight developments: central-bank speak, major data, geopolitics
  □ Read the RISK REGIME: stocks, VIX, bond yields, havens (Ch. 5–6)
  □ Note anything that CHANGES a currency's scorecard → update the bias
  □ Plan around today's events (what to avoid, what to trade after)

── WEEKLY (1–2 hrs) ──────────────────────────────────────────
  □ Review each currency's SCORECARD; update factors from the week's data
  □ Re-read your THESES: still valid? strengthening or weakening?
  □ Map the WEEK AHEAD: central-bank meetings, CPI/NFP/GDP/PMIs to come
  □ Rank your pairs by conviction; pick where you'll focus

── PER EVENT (around major releases) ─────────────────────────
  □ Know the forecast (what's priced in) & what would surprise
  □ Note the actual vs forecast and, more importantly, the market's REACTION (Ch. 4)
  □ Update the scorecard if the data shifts the policy path

── MONTHLY (deeper) ──────────────────────────────────────────
  □ Step back: what are the dominant THEMES this month/quarter?
  □ Which central banks are diverging? Where is the cleanest strong-vs-weak pairing?
  □ Review how your macro calls performed (feed the Vol 4 journal/KPIs)
```

> 🔑 You are not trying to *read everything* — you're trying to keep a small set of **scorecards and theses current.** Filter the firehose of news through one question: *"does this change my bias for a currency I trade?"* If not, it's noise — ignore it.

## 7.4 Where to study — sources (and how to use them)

```
ESSENTIAL & FREE:
  • Economic calendar (ForexFactory, Investing.com, broker) — events, forecasts, impact
  • Reputable financial news (for central-bank decisions, data, geopolitics)
  • Central-bank websites — the PRIMARY source: statements, minutes, speeches, projections
  • Bond-yield & rate-expectation data; commodity & equity index/VIX quotes (Ch. 6)

VALUABLE:
  • Central-bank meeting minutes & the "dot plot" (Fed) — the policy path in their words
  • Market commentary from credible macro analysts (to pressure-test your view, not replace it)
  • Positioning data (e.g. COT report) — what the crowd is already long/short
```

**How to consume sources like a pro:**
- **Go to the primary source** for central banks (the statement/minutes/speech), not just headlines about them.
- **Read to update your scorecard**, not to be entertained. Have a purpose.
- **Pressure-test, don't outsource.** Use others' analysis to *challenge* your thesis, never to replace your own reasoning. ⚠️ Beware "finfluencer" noise and anyone selling certainty (the scam warning from Vol 1 applies to macro gurus too).
- **Track the consensus** (what's priced in) as diligently as the data itself (Ch. 4).

---

## 7.5 Focus — depth over breadth

The top 1% are not generalists spread across 40 pairs. They go **deep on a few:**

- **Specialize.** Pick **2–4 currencies / a few pairs** and know them *cold* — their central banks, their data calendar, their drivers, their personality. Deep beats wide.
- **Master your central banks.** Since policy is the dominant driver, truly understanding 2–3 central banks' reaction functions is worth more than shallow awareness of all of them.
- **Build over time.** Macro fluency compounds — each cycle you trade deepens your feel for how these currencies react. Treat it as a multi-year apprenticeship (Vol 5's career mindset).

> 🔑 **Becoming top 1% in macro is about depth, not coverage.** A trader who deeply understands the Fed, the ECB, and EUR/USD's drivers will out-trade someone with a superficial view of every currency. Narrow your focus and go deep.

---

## 7.6 Integrate macro into your system (tie to Vols 3 & 4)

Your macro research is the **bias-generation layer** of your trading system (Vol 4). It plugs in cleanly:

```
MACRO RESEARCH (this volume)  →  produces a directional BIAS per pair
        +
TECHNICAL ANALYSIS (Vol 3)    →  times the ENTRY at a quality level in the bias direction
        +
RISK & SYSTEM (Vols 1, 4)     →  sizes, sets the stop, manages the trade, journals it
        =
A complete, macro-informed, technically-timed, risk-managed trade.
```

Record your macro bias and reasoning **in your journal** (Vol 4) alongside each trade. Over time your KPIs will reveal whether your macro reads actually add edge — and which kinds of macro setups work best for you. *Measure your macro, just like everything else.*

## 7.7 Chapter summary

```
• The deliverable of research is a clear, current BIAS per currency, with reasoning — not
  "staying informed." If it doesn't end in a bias, it's just news consumption.
• Maintain a CURRENCY SCORECARD (policy, rates, inflation, growth, jobs, risk-fit,
  intermarket, geopolitics) → a net bias; COMPARE two scorecards for a pair bias.
• Write a one-paragraph THESIS per pair, with what would INVALIDATE it — for conviction
  to hold and a trigger to flip.
• Run a RESEARCH ROUTINE: daily (calendar + regime + scorecard updates), weekly (theses +
  week ahead), per-event (surprise + reaction), monthly (themes + review).
• Use primary sources (central banks), track the CONSENSUS, pressure-test don't outsource,
  ignore guru noise. Filter all news through "does this change my bias?"
• FOCUS: go deep on 2–4 currencies / a few pairs and master their central banks. Depth > breadth.
• Macro = the BIAS layer; combine with Vol 3 timing and Vol 4 risk/journaling. Measure your macro.
```
