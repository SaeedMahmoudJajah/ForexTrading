# Chapter 3 — Your Edge & Strategy

---

This is where you define *exactly what you trade* — your edge. It integrates the macro bias of Volume 6, the technical method of Volume 3, and the strategy-specification discipline of Volume 4 into a concrete, written set of setups. Vague edges ("I trade price action") produce vague, unmeasurable results. This chapter forces the precision that makes an edge real.

> 🔑 **Your edge must be specific enough to write down, test, and execute identically every time** (Vol 4). If you can't state it in clear rules a stranger could follow, you don't have an edge yet — you have a feeling. This chapter turns the feeling into a system.

---

## 3.1 What "your edge" means

Your **edge** is a defined, positive-expectancy way of trading — a small set of repeatable setups, traded with risk discipline, that produce more profit than loss over many trades (Vol 4). It is *not* a secret indicator or a prediction method. For you, it's the answer to:

```
• WHICH markets do I trade? (a focused few)
• WHAT is my directional BIAS method? (macro — Vol 6)
• WHAT are my specific SETUPS? (technical — Vol 3, tested — Vol 4)
• HOW do I combine bias + setup into a trade?
```

---

## 3.2 Choose your markets (focus over breadth)

From Volumes 1, 3, and 6: **specialize.** Pick a small, focused set of pairs and know them deeply.

```
□ Trade 1–3 pairs to start (majors recommended — tightest spreads, most liquid, e.g. EUR/USD).
□ Match pairs to your style & sessions (Vol 1) and to where you can form a macro view (Vol 6).
□ Avoid spreading across many pairs — you can't track macro or master the behavior of 20 pairs.
□ Know each pair's "personality": volatility, best session, key drivers, what moves it.
```

> 🔑 Depth beats breadth (Vol 6). A trader who knows EUR/USD's drivers, central banks, and technical behavior *cold* will out-trade someone shallow across 20 pairs. Your plan names your focused markets and why.

---

## 3.3 Define your directional bias method (macro — Vol 6)

How you'll decide *which way* to trade each pair, before any chart setup:

```
□ Maintain a CURRENCY SCORECARD per currency (Vol 6, Ch. 7): policy path, rates, inflation,
  growth, risk-sentiment fit, intermarket, geopolitics → a net bias.
□ Form a PAIR BIAS by comparing the two currencies' scorecards (strong vs weak).
□ Keep a one-paragraph THESIS per pair, with what would invalidate it (Vol 6).
□ Update via your research routine (daily/weekly — Ch. 5).
□ RULE: only take setups in the direction of my macro bias (bias-as-filter, Vol 6, Ch. 8).
```

This is the layer most retail traders skip — and it's a core part of the top-1% edge. Your bias method belongs in your written plan.

> 🔑 **Macro bias + technical setup = the professional discretionary method** (Vol 6). The macro keeps you on the right side; the setup times the entry. Define both in your plan, and require them to *agree* before you trade.

---

## 3.4 Define your specific setups (technical — Vol 3, tested — Vol 4)

Now the concrete trades. For **each** setup you trade, write a full specification (Vol 4's spec sheet). Most traders need only **2–3 well-mastered setups** — not fifty.

```
SETUP SPEC (one per setup):
  NAME: ____________________ (e.g. "Trend pullback to demand zone + golden pocket")
  MARKET CONDITION needed: trending / ranging / breakout (Vol 3)
  MACRO ALIGNMENT: must agree with my pair bias (Vol 6)
  ENTRY RULES (exact, all must be true):
    • Structure/context: __________________________ (Vol 3, Ch. 3)
    • Location/level: ____________________________ (Vol 3, Ch. 4 — S/R, zones)
    • Confluence: ________________________________ (Vol 3, Ch. 7 — Fib, MA, etc.)
    • Trigger: ___________________________________ (Vol 3, Ch. 2 — candle/CHoCH)
  STOP RULE: ___________________ (beyond invalidation + buffer; ATR-based — Vol 3)
  TARGET RULE: _________________ (next level / measured move / Fib ext.) — min R:R ≥ ____
  TRADE MANAGEMENT: break-even at ____R | partials | trailing rule
  WHAT INVALIDATES IT: _________________________________________________
```

> 🔑 **Two or three setups you've mastered and journaled beat a vague knowledge of fifty** (Vol 3, Ch. 8; Vol 4). Mastery is narrow and deep. Your plan lists your specific setups — and you trade *only* those.

---

## 3.5 Prove the edge before you trust it (Vol 4)

A setup isn't part of your edge until the **data** says it works. Before a setup earns real-money size:

```
1. BACKTEST it (Vol 4, Ch. 4): apply the rules to history; measure expectancy. Avoid the
   biases (curve-fitting, etc.); validate out-of-sample.
2. FORWARD-TEST on demo (Vol 4, Ch. 5): trade it live-market, journaled, for weeks.
3. CHECK the numbers: positive expectancy over a meaningful sample (100+ trades, Vol 4, Ch. 3)?
4. ONLY THEN trade it live small (Vol 5: micro size, 0.5% risk), still journaling.
```

> 🔑 You don't *assume* your edge works because it looks good — you *prove* it with journaled data (Vol 4). An "edge" you've never measured is a hope. Your plan should state your acceptance criteria and only include *validated* setups.

---

## 3.6 The complete trade — bias + setup + risk, integrated

Your edge in action chains the layers (this is what goes in your plan as your method):

```
1. BIAS:     Macro scorecard/thesis (Vol 6) → pair bias = long or short.
2. REGIME:   Risk-on/off supports it? Intermarket confluence? (Vol 6)
3. SETUP:    Price reaches my setup's conditions IN the bias direction (Vol 3).
4. TRIGGER:  My entry trigger fires at a quality level (Vol 3).
5. RISK:     Stop at invalidation; size = my fixed % (Ch. 4); reward ≥ 2R.
6. EVENT:    No high-impact news imminent (Vol 6, Ch. 4).
7. EXECUTE:  Calm, by the plan, SL+TP attached. Manage & journal (Vol 4).
```

When macro bias, risk regime, technical setup, and trigger **all align**, that's your highest-conviction trade — size toward your maximum *within your risk rules* (Vol 2's conviction sizing).

---

## 3.7 Chapter summary

```
• Your EDGE = a focused set of markets + a macro bias method + 2–3 tested setups + risk
  discipline, producing positive expectancy over many trades. Not a prediction or a secret.
• FOCUS your markets (1–3 majors to start); know them deeply. Depth > breadth.
• Define your BIAS method (Vol 6 scorecard/thesis) and require setups to align with it.
• Specify each SETUP fully (Vol 4 spec): condition, macro alignment, entry rules (structure,
  level, confluence, trigger), stop, target, management, invalidation. Master 2–3, not 50.
• PROVE each setup (backtest → forward-test → positive expectancy over 100+ trades) before
  trusting it with real size. An unmeasured edge is a hope.
• Your trade chains the layers: bias → regime → setup → trigger → risk → event check →
  execute & journal. Full alignment = highest conviction.
```

You know what you'll trade. Now the layer that keeps you alive long enough to profit from it: risk.
