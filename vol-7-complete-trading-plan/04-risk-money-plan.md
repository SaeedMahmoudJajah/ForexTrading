# Chapter 4 — Your Risk & Money Management Plan

[← Your Edge & Strategy](03-edge-strategy.md) | [Volume 7 Home](README.md) | [Next: Your Operating Routine →](05-operating-routine.md)

---

This is the most important section of your entire trading plan. Your edge finds opportunities; your **risk management determines whether you survive long enough for that edge to pay off.** It integrates the survival rules from Volume 1, the sizing math from Volume 4, and the prop discipline from Volume 5 into the hard, non-negotiable rules of your operation. Everything else can be good — but get this wrong and you blow up regardless.

> 🔑 **Capital preservation is the job; profit is the byproduct** (Vol 2). Most traders fail not because they can't find trades, but because one or two oversized trades destroy them. Your risk plan exists to make sure a single trade — or a single bad day — *can never* take you out. Write these rules as laws, not suggestions.

---

## 4.1 The non-negotiable risk rules (your laws)

These are the core rules from across the series. Adopt them, fill in your numbers, and treat them as inviolable:

```
1. RISK PER TRADE:     Risk ≤ ____% of my account per trade (≤1%; start 0.5%). (Vol 1)
2. ALWAYS A STOP:      Every trade has a stop loss, set BEFORE entry, at invalidation. (Vol 1)
3. NEVER WIDEN A STOP: I never move a stop further away to avoid a loss. (Vol 1)
4. SIZE TO THE STOP:   Position sized so the stop = my fixed % risk. (Vol 1, 4)
5. MIN REWARD:RISK:    I only take trades with R:R ≥ ____ (≥1.5–2). (Vol 1, 3)
6. NEVER ADD TO LOSERS: I never average down into a losing position. (Vol 2)
7. DAILY LOSS LIMIT:   Down ____% on the day → I STOP trading. (Vol 5)
8. WEEKLY LOSS LIMIT:  Down ____% on the week → I STOP & review. (Vol 1, 5)
9. MAX OPEN RISK:      Total risk across open trades ≤ ____% (~3–5%, correlation-aware). (Vol 1)
10. RISK CAPITAL ONLY: I never trade money I can't afford to lose. (Vol 1)
```

> 🔑 These ten rules, *actually followed*, are most of what keeps the 1% in the game and the 99% out. They're simple to write and hard to obey under pressure — which is exactly why they're written, visible, and treated as laws (Vol 1's commandments).

## 4.2 Position sizing — your formula (Vol 1, 4)

The mechanism that converts your fixed % risk + stop distance into the correct trade size:

```
Position size (lots) = (Account × Risk%) ÷ (Stop distance in pips × Pip value per lot)

YOUR RULE:
  □ I use a position-size calculator on EVERY trade — never eyeball it.
  □ Risk % stays FIXED; the wider my stop, the smaller my position (and vice versa).
  □ My risk per trade in $ at my current account size = $______
```

This single discipline — constant fractional risk — means no single trade can hurt you disproportionately, and it auto-compounds as the account grows / auto-protects as it shrinks (Vol 4's fixed-fractional model).

## 4.3 Drawdown rules & circuit-breakers (Vol 1, 5)

Limits on *yourself*, to stop emotion from compounding losses:

```
□ PERSONAL DAILY STOP:   −____% → close the platform for the day. (stops revenge trading)
□ MAX LOSING TRADES/DAY: ____ losers → done for the day, regardless of P/L.
□ WEEKLY STOP:           −____% → stop for the week, review the journal.
□ DRAWDOWN PAUSE:        Account down ____% from peak → reduce size, review, don't push.
□ POST-STREAK RULE:      After a big winning OR losing streak → stay level, don't change size
                         on emotion. (Vol 2 — flatten euphoria and despair)
```

If trading a prop/funded account (Vol 5), set these **personal limits well inside the firm's hard limits** — so the firm's drawdown floor is never even threatened (Vol 5, Ch. 4).

> 🔑 The daily loss limit is your most important circuit-breaker: it removes the *option* to spiral. You cannot revenge-trade an account you've closed for the day. Pre-commit to it in writing and obey it like a law (Vol 5, Ch. 7).

## 4.4 Capital allocation & survival (Vol 4, 5)

How you deploy and protect your capital at the portfolio level:

```
□ TOTAL RISK CAPITAL: $______ (money I can fully afford to lose)
□ ALLOCATION: own account $____ | prop evaluation fees budget $____ (a business cost, Vol 5)
□ EMERGENCY RUNWAY: ____ months of living expenses in CASH, OUTSIDE trading (Vol 5, Ch. 8)
□ NEVER let a single firm/account hold money I can't lose access to (Vol 5)
□ WITHDRAW & DIVERSIFY profits out of trading accounts regularly (Vol 5, Ch. 8)
□ CORRELATION AWARENESS: treat correlated positions as ONE bet; size the theme (Vol 1, 6)
```

### The survival math you must respect (Vol 1, 4)
```
Drawdown    Gain needed to recover
  −10%        +11%
  −25%        +33%
  −50%       +100%      ← why you protect capital obsessively
  −75%       +300%
A small risk per trade + positive expectancy = risk of ruin near zero (Vol 4).
```

## 4.5 Make your risk rules personal (Vol 7, Ch. 2)

Generic rules are good; rules tailored to *your* weaknesses (Ch. 2) are better. Add custom rules that defend *your* specific failure modes:

```
EXAMPLES (write yours):
  • "I revenge trade" → hard −2% daily stop + a mandatory 30-min break after any loss.
  • "I overtrade when bored" → max ____ trades per day; A+ setups only.
  • "I oversize on 'sure things'" → my % risk is FIXED; conviction never breaks the limit (Vol 2).
  • "I move stops" → I use a hard stop order set at entry and don't touch it except toward profit.
```

> 🔑 The market exploits *your* specific weaknesses (Ch. 2). The most valuable risk rules in your plan are the ones written specifically to defend against how *you* personally blow up.

## 4.6 Chapter summary

```
• Risk management is the MOST IMPORTANT part of your plan — it decides survival, and
  survival is what lets your edge pay off. Capital preservation is the job.
• Adopt the 10 non-negotiable LAWS: ≤1% risk/trade, always a stop, never widen it, size to
  the stop, R:R ≥ 1.5–2, never add to losers, daily & weekly loss limits, max open risk,
  risk capital only.
• POSITION SIZE = (Account × Risk%) ÷ (stop pips × pip value). Use a calculator every time;
  fixed % risk auto-compounds and auto-protects.
• Set CIRCUIT-BREAKERS: personal daily stop, max losers/day, weekly stop, drawdown pause,
  post-streak discipline. On prop accounts, set them INSIDE the firm's limits (Vol 5).
• Manage CAPITAL: risk capital only, budget prop fees, keep a cash runway outside trading,
  withdraw/diversify, treat correlated trades as one bet. Respect the drawdown-recovery math.
• PERSONALIZE rules to defend YOUR specific weaknesses — those are the most valuable ones.
```

Your edge and your risk rules are set. Now the routine that executes them, day in and day out.
