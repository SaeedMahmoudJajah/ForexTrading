# Chapter 5 — Your Operating Routine

---

A plan is only as good as its **daily execution.** Your routine is the engine that runs your operating system — the repeatable sequence that combines macro research (Vol 6), technical prep (Vol 3), disciplined execution, and journaling (Vol 4) into a professional's day. This is what makes everything else *happen*, consistently, instead of depending on mood and willpower (Vol 5).

> 🔑 **The routine IS the discipline.** When your process is fixed and written, you don't rely on in-the-moment willpower — you just follow the checklist. Champions are consistent because their routine is consistent (Vol 5). Your plan's routine is what turns intentions into actions, every day.

---

## 5.1 The pre-market routine (before you trade)

Preparation is where the edge is built — before a single trade. Adapt the timing to your style and sessions (Vol 1/2).

```
PRE-MARKET CHECKLIST:
  STATE (Vol 5, 7):
    □ Am I rested, calm, and clear? (if not → size down or sit out)
    □ Mindset check: no tilt, no FOMO, no pressure to "make money today"
  MACRO (Vol 6):
    □ Scan the economic calendar — high-impact events today/this week? plan around them
    □ Check overnight: central-bank speak, major data, geopolitics
    □ Read the risk regime: stocks, VIX, yields, havens
    □ Update currency scorecards/theses if anything changed → confirm today's bias
  TECHNICAL (Vol 3):
    □ Top-down review of my pairs: HTF trend & structure, mark key levels/zones
    □ Identify which of my setups could trigger today and where
  RISK (Vol 4, 7):
    □ Confirm today's risk per trade, daily loss limit, and max trades
    □ (Prop) Note my distance-to-floor and personal limits (Vol 5)
  PLAN THE DAY:
    □ Write today's watchlist: pairs, biases, the specific setups I'm hunting
```

---

## 5.2 During the session (execution)

Now you *execute the plan you already made* — you don't improvise.

```
DURING-SESSION RULES:
  □ Trade ONLY my planned A+ setups, aligned with my macro bias. No improvising. (Vol 3, 6)
  □ Every trade: SL + TP attached, sized to my fixed % risk. (Vol 1, 4)
  □ Check the full chain before entry: bias → regime → setup → trigger → risk → event. (Ch. 3)
  □ Track risk: respect daily loss limit, max-trades, and (prop) distance-to-floor. (Vol 4, 5)
  □ Once in a trade: manage per plan (break-even, trail, scale). Don't fiddle. (Vol 4)
  □ Hit my daily loss limit OR my target → DONE. Close the platform. (Vol 5)
  □ No setup = no trade. Sitting on my hands is the job, not a failure. (Vol 2)
```

> 🔑 The session is for **execution, not analysis.** The thinking was done pre-market when you were calm. During live trading, your job is to follow the plan and resist the urge to do *something* just to feel active. Overtrading from boredom is the #1 occupational hazard (Vol 5, Ch. 6).

---

## 5.3 The post-session routine (capture & learn)

The session isn't over until it's recorded. This is where improvement is born (Vol 4).

```
POST-SESSION CHECKLIST:
  □ Journal EVERY trade while fresh (Vol 4 schema): plan, R-multiple, confluence, emotion,
    and the key field — "Did I follow my plan?"
  □ Self-grade on PROCESS, not P/L (Vol 2): did I follow my rules today?
  □ Note one lesson; flag anything for the weekly review
  □ Note any rule breaks honestly — these are gold for fixing your weaknesses (Ch. 2)
  □ Step away. Close the laptop. Trading is done for the day.
```

---

## 5.4 The weekly & monthly rhythm

Zooming out keeps the system healthy and improving (Vol 4, 6).

```
WEEKLY REVIEW (~30–60 min):
  □ Compute KPIs: win%, avg R, expectancy, profit factor, max drawdown (Vol 4, Ch. 7)
  □ "% of trades I followed my plan" — the discipline metric
  □ Review macro scorecards/theses; map next week's events (Vol 6)
  □ Best decision & worst mistake of the week; ONE adjustment for next week
  □ Rank my pairs by conviction for the week ahead

MONTHLY REVIEW (deeper):
  □ Full KPI review + equity curve + segmentation by setup/session/pair (Vol 4, Ch. 7)
  □ Which setups/conditions to keep, cut, or scale? (subtraction is an edge)
  □ Are my macro themes still valid? (Vol 6)
  □ Progress vs my goals & milestones (Ch. 7); scaling decisions (Vol 5)
  □ Update the plan itself if the DATA justifies it (Ch. 8)
```

> 🔑 The PLAN → EXECUTE → RECORD → REVIEW → REFINE loop (Vol 4) is the heartbeat of your operation. Daily execution and journaling feed the weekly/monthly reviews, which feed back into refining the plan. **This loop, run for years, is how skill compounds and how you reach the top 1%.**

## 5.5 Building the routine into your life (Vol 5)

A routine only works if it's sustainable. From the full-time discipline of Volume 5:

```
□ FIXED TRADING HOURS: I trade during ____ (my edge's best session); I'm OFF otherwise.
  The market is open 24/5 — I am not.
□ DEDICATED WORKSPACE: a real "work mode" environment, minimal distractions.
□ ATHLETE'S BASICS: sleep, exercise, nutrition — they protect my decision quality (Vol 5).
□ ENVIRONMENT HYGIENE: clean charts, muted hype groups, written limits visible at my desk.
□ BOUNDARIES: clear separation of trading time and life — to avoid burnout & overtrading.
```

> 🔑 Going full-time tempts you to "always be trading." Resist it. **Your job is to be prepared and selective, not busy** (Vol 5, Ch. 6). A sustainable routine with clear boundaries is what lets you do this for years without burning out — and longevity is how you become a top trader.

## 5.6 Chapter summary

```
• The ROUTINE is the discipline — a fixed, written process replaces in-the-moment willpower.
• PRE-MARKET: check your state, do macro prep (calendar, regime, scorecards — Vol 6),
  technical prep (top-down, levels, setups — Vol 3), confirm risk limits, write the watchlist.
• DURING: EXECUTE only — planned A+ setups, full chain checked, SL+TP, risk respected, manage
  per plan, stop at daily limit/target. No improvising; no-setup = no-trade.
• POST: journal every trade fresh (esp. "did I follow my plan?"), self-grade on PROCESS,
  note lessons & rule-breaks, then step away.
• WEEKLY/MONTHLY: compute KPIs, review macro & setups, segment results, track goals, refine
  the plan on DATA. The PLAN→EXECUTE→RECORD→REVIEW→REFINE loop is your heartbeat.
• Build it sustainably: fixed hours, dedicated space, athlete's basics, clean environment,
  clear boundaries. Be prepared & selective, not busy.
```
