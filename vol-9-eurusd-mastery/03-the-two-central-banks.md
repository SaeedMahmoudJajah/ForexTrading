# Chapter 3 — The Two Central Banks

[← The Drivers of EUR/USD](02-drivers.md) | [Volume 9 Home](README.md) | [Next: The EUR/USD Economic Calendar →](04-economic-calendar.md)

---

EUR/USD is, above all, a bet on **the Federal Reserve versus the European Central Bank.** Their relative policy paths are the engine of the pair (Ch. 2). To master EUR/USD, you must understand both institutions deeply: how they're structured, what they target, how they communicate, and how to read the divergence between them. This is the heart of the pair.

---

## 3.1 The Federal Reserve (the Fed) — the dollar side

```
WHO       The central bank of the United States; the most important on earth (Vol 6, Ch. 2)
BODY      The Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) sets policy
MEETINGS  8 scheduled per year (~every 6 weeks)
MANDATE   DUAL mandate: price stability (~2% inflation) AND maximum employment
KEY RATE  The federal funds rate (a target range)
SIGNALS   The post-meeting statement, the Chair's press conference, the "dot plot"
          (policymakers' rate projections), meeting minutes, and official speeches
```

**Reading the Fed:**
- The **dual mandate** means the Fed weighs *both* inflation and jobs — so both US inflation data *and* the jobs report (NFP) move Fed expectations and therefore EUR/USD (Ch. 4).
- The **"dot plot"** (released quarterly) shows where each policymaker expects rates to go — a key map of the *expected path* (Vol 6, Ch. 2).
- The **Chair's press conference** often moves markets more than the decision itself — tone (hawkish/dovish) is scrutinized word by word.
- Because the dollar is the world's reserve currency, **the Fed is the gravitational center** — its moves ripple through everything, EUR/USD included (Vol 8, Ch. 2).

---

## 3.2 The European Central Bank (the ECB) — the euro side

```
WHO       The central bank for the 20-country Eurozone
BODY      The Governing Council sets policy
MEETINGS  8 per year (~every 6 weeks)
MANDATE   PRIMARY mandate: price stability (inflation target ~2%) — note: NOT a dual mandate
KEY RATE  The deposit facility rate is the main policy rate markets watch
SIGNALS   The policy statement, the President's press conference, the accounts (minutes),
          staff projections, and speeches by Governing Council members
```

**Reading the ECB:**
- **Single mandate (price stability):** unlike the Fed, the ECB's primary legal focus is inflation — so **Eurozone inflation (HICP)** is the dominant data for ECB expectations (Ch. 4).
- **One policy for 20 economies:** the ECB must set a single policy for diverse members, creating internal tension between **"hawks"** (traditionally Germany/northern Europe) and **"doves"** (often southern Europe). This makes ECB communication and its internal balance especially important to read.
- **Fragmentation tools:** the ECB must also guard against periphery stress (widening BTP–Bund spreads, Ch. 2) — a complication the Fed doesn't face.
- The **President's press conference** is the key communication event, parsed for hawkish/dovish shifts just like the Fed's.

---

## 3.3 Fed vs ECB — the comparison that drives the pair

| | Federal Reserve | European Central Bank |
|---|-----------------|------------------------|
| **Covers** | One unified economy (US) | 20 economies (Eurozone) |
| **Mandate** | **Dual** (inflation + employment) | **Primary: price stability** |
| **Key rate** | Fed funds target range | Deposit facility rate |
| **Key data it watches** | US CPI/PCE **and** jobs (NFP) | Eurozone HICP inflation |
| **Extra concern** | — | Fragmentation / periphery risk |
| **Communication** | Statement, presser, **dot plot**, minutes | Statement, presser, projections, accounts |

> 🔑 The asymmetry matters for trading: because the Fed has a **dual mandate**, US *jobs* data moves the dollar strongly; because the ECB is **single-mandate**, Eurozone *inflation* is the ECB's dominant trigger. Knowing which data each bank cares most about tells you which releases will move EUR/USD hardest from each side (Ch. 4).

## 3.4 The divergence trade — the core of EUR/USD

EUR/USD's primary trend is set by which bank is **more hawkish relative to what's priced in** (Ch. 2, Vol 6, Ch. 2):

```
THE DIVERGENCE LADDER (what moves EUR/USD):
  Fed turning MORE hawkish than expected, ECB steady/dovish  →  EUR/USD DOWN
  ECB turning MORE hawkish than expected, Fed steady/dovish  →  EUR/USD UP
  Both hawkish, but Fed MORE so                              →  EUR/USD DOWN (gap widens for USD)
  Both easing, but ECB MORE so                               →  EUR/USD DOWN
  → It's always the RELATIVE shift in the GAP, vs expectations, that matters.
```

**Classic regimes (illustrative, from history):**
- **2014–2015:** Fed moving toward tightening while the ECB launched QE → **EUR/USD fell sharply** (divergence favoring the dollar).
- **2021–2022:** Fed hiked aggressively to fight inflation while the ECB lagged → **EUR/USD fell below parity.**
- **Periods when the ECB "caught up"** to the Fed have driven euro recoveries.

> 🔑 **To form your EUR/USD bias, build a "divergence read":** Is the Fed or the ECB shifting more hawkish/dovish *relative to what's priced in*? That single comparison, kept current, is the backbone of trading the pair (Vol 6, Ch. 7). Everything else (data, yields, growth) is evidence that updates it.

## 3.5 How to read the central banks in practice

A practical routine for tracking the two banks (feeds your scorecard, Ch. 8; Vol 6, Ch. 7):

```
FOR EACH BANK, TRACK:
  □ Current policy rate and the EXPECTED path (hiking / holding / cutting cycle)
  □ The next meeting date and what the market has PRICED IN
  □ The tone trend: getting more hawkish or dovish? (statements, pressers, speeches)
  □ The data it cares most about (Fed: US inflation + jobs; ECB: Eurozone inflation)

THEN COMPARE:
  □ Who is more hawkish/dovish RELATIVE to expectations? → that's your divergence bias.
  □ Does the US–German yield spread confirm it? (Ch. 2 — the real-time tell)
```

- **Listen to the pressers and speeches**, not just the decisions — tone shifts are where the moves come from (Vol 6, Ch. 2).
- **Track the priced-in path** (rate-expectation tools, the dot plot, economist forecasts) so you know what would be a *surprise* (Vol 6, Ch. 4).
- **Watch for "pivots"** — when a bank signals a change of cycle (e.g. from hiking to cutting). Pivots drive the biggest EUR/USD trends.

---

## 3.6 Central-bank event risk

The two banks' meetings and the Chair/President speeches are the **highest-impact scheduled events** for EUR/USD (Ch. 4; Vol 6, Ch. 8):

- **FOMC and ECB decision days** produce sharp volatility — especially the *press conferences*. Expect fast, two-way moves and slippage.
- **Default to caution:** reduce size or stand aside *into* these events, and trade the clearer digested move *after* (Vol 6, Ch. 8) — unless you're an experienced trader deliberately positioning on a strong thesis.
- **Speeches between meetings** (Fed Chair, ECB President, and key officials) can move the pair when they shift the expected path.

> 🔑 The Fed and ECB are the two most important dates on your EUR/USD calendar. Know exactly when they meet, know what's priced in, and respect the volatility (Ch. 4, 7). These events *set the divergence* that drives the pair for weeks afterward.

## 3.7 Chapter summary

```
• EUR/USD is fundamentally a bet on the FED vs the ECB. Their relative policy path is the engine.
• FED: dual mandate (inflation + jobs), 8 meetings/yr, fed funds rate, dot plot; the world's
  gravitational central bank. US inflation AND jobs move it.
• ECB: primary mandate price stability, 8 meetings/yr, deposit rate; sets ONE policy for 20
  economies (hawk/dove tension) and guards against fragmentation. Eurozone INFLATION (HICP) is
  its dominant trigger.
• The asymmetry tells you what moves the pair from each side: US jobs (Fed) vs Eurozone inflation (ECB).
• The DIVERGENCE TRADE: whichever bank turns more hawkish RELATIVE to what's priced in sets
  EUR/USD's direction. Most big trends are Fed-vs-ECB divergence stories. Watch for PIVOTS.
• Track each bank's rate, path, tone, and the priced-in expectations; confirm with the US–German
  yield spread. FOMC/ECB days & pressers are the highest-impact events — respect them.
```
