## 8.1 Your EUR/USD scorecard — the bias engine

The heart of your EUR/USD process is a **divergence scorecard** that produces your directional bias (Ch. 2–3; Vol 6, Ch. 7). Maintain it and update it as data arrives:

```
EUR/USD BIAS SCORECARD                                        Date: ______
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┬──────────────────────┐
│ FACTOR                                        │  EUR-side  USD-side   │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────┤
│ Monetary policy path (ECB vs Fed, vs priced)  │   ____      ____      │ ← biggest weight (Ch. 3)
│ Rate / yield differential (US–German spread)  │         ____          │   (Ch. 2)
│ Inflation trend (HICP vs US CPI/PCE)          │   ____      ____      │
│ Growth & PMIs (Eurozone vs US)                │   ____      ____      │
│ Risk regime / dollar smile side               │         ____          │   (Ch. 2)
│ Eurozone-specific risk (periphery, energy)    │   ____                │   (Ch. 2)
└──────────────────────────────────────────────┴──────────────────────┘
NET EUR/USD BIAS:  ☐ Bullish  ☐ Bearish  ☐ Neutral/range     Conviction: low/med/high
THESIS (1–2 sentences): ___________________________________________________
WHAT WOULD FLIP IT: ______________________________________________________
```

> 🔑 This scorecard *is* your EUR/USD edge's foundation: it forces you to weigh the Fed-vs-ECB divergence and the supporting evidence into one clear bias (Ch. 3). Update it as releases hit (Ch. 4), and only take setups in its direction (Ch. 6). A current, written bias is what separates trading the pair with conviction from guessing.

## 8.2 Your daily EUR/USD routine

A specialized version of Volume 7's routine (Ch. 5 there), built for this pair:

```
PRE-MARKET (before the London session — Ch. 7):
  □ State check: calm, rested, clear? (Vol 7, Ch. 6)
  □ Update the SCORECARD with any overnight data/speeches → confirm today's bias (8.1)
  □ Check BOTH calendars (US + EZ) for today's high-impact events & times (Ch. 4)
  □ Check the US–German yield spread & DXY for confirmation/divergence (Ch. 2)
  □ Mark KEY LEVELS on Daily/H4: round numbers, S/R zones, range boundaries (Ch. 5)
  □ Mark the Asian-session range high/low (London breakout fuel — Ch. 6)
  □ Define today's plan: bias, which setups you're hunting, max trades, max loss (Vol 7, Ch. 4)

DURING (London & the overlap — prime time, Ch. 7):
  □ Trade ONLY with-bias setups at your marked levels (Ch. 6); A+ only
  □ Full chain: bias → regime → setup → trigger → risk → event check (Ch. 6)
  □ SL + TP attached; size ≤1% (Vol 1); respect daily loss limit & max trades (Vol 7, Ch. 4)
  □ Stand aside into high-impact releases; trade the digested move after (Ch. 6, Strategy 4)
  □ Hit target or daily limit → DONE (Vol 5/7)

POST-SESSION:
  □ Journal every trade (Vol 4): the macro bias, setup, R-multiple, "did I follow my plan?"
  □ Update the scorecard if the day's data shifted the divergence
  □ Note one lesson

WEEKLY:
  □ Review KPIs & % plan-followed (Vol 4); map next week's US/EZ catalysts (Ch. 4)
  □ Re-assess the divergence thesis; rank conviction for the week
```

## 8.3 Your EUR/USD watchlist — what to keep on screen

Because you're mastering this one pair, watch the specific things that drive it:

```
PRIMARY:    EUR/USD price (Daily, H4, M15) + DXY (the inverse mirror, Ch. 1)
MACRO TELL: US–German 2-yr & 10-yr yield spread (the divergence's real-time tell, Ch. 2)
REGIME:     Risk gauges — equities/VIX (which side of the dollar smile, Ch. 2)
EURO RISK:  The Italian BTP–German Bund spread (Eurozone-stress gauge, Ch. 2) — in stress regimes
CALENDAR:   Both US & EZ economic calendars + the FOMC/ECB schedule (Ch. 4)
LEVELS:     Your marked round numbers, S/R zones, range boundaries, Asian range (Ch. 5)
```

> 🔑 This focused watchlist is the advantage of single-pair mastery: instead of monitoring 20 markets shallowly, you watch the **handful of things that actually drive EUR/USD** — the yield spread, DXY, the risk regime, the calendar, and your levels — deeply and continuously (Vol 6, Ch. 7).

## 8.5 The path to EUR/USD mastery

Mastery is built over time, the same disciplined way as everything in this series (Vol 4, 5, 7):

```
1. STUDY this volume; build your scorecard and watchlist (8.1, 8.3).
2. OBSERVE EUR/USD daily — watch how it reacts to the Fed/ECB, data, yields, and sessions.
   Keep a running read of the divergence (Ch. 3) and note what actually moves it.
3. DEMO-TRADE your EUR/USD strategies (Ch. 6), journaled, until positive expectancy (Vol 4).
4. GO LIVE small (Vol 5): micro size, 0.5% risk, same process.
5. REVIEW & REFINE: which EUR/USD setups/sessions work best for you? (Vol 4, Ch. 7; segment your data)
6. DEEPEN over years: every Fed/ECB cycle you trade sharpens your feel for the pair.
```

> 🔑 **Single-pair mastery compounds.** Every central-bank cycle, every CPI, every range and breakout you trade and journal on EUR/USD deepens your feel for it. In a few years of focused study you can know this one pair better than most traders know any market — and that depth, executed with discipline, is a genuine top-tier edge (Vol 6, Ch. 7).
