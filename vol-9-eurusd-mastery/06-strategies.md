Now we turn understanding into concrete trades. This chapter presents specific strategies tuned to EUR/USD's personality (Ch. 5), each filtered by the macro bias (Ch. 2–3) and governed by the risk discipline of the series. These are **educational example setups to learn structure from** — not guarantees. Backtest and forward-test any of them (Vol 4) before risking real money.

> 🔑 **Every strategy here is filtered by your EUR/USD macro bias** (the Fed-vs-ECB divergence, Ch. 3). You take *with-bias* setups preferentially — long setups when your bias is bullish, short setups when bearish. Macro tells you *which way*; these setups tell you *where and when* (Vol 6, Ch. 8). That fusion is the core method.

---

## 6.1 The universal filter — bias + risk (applies to every setup below)

```
BEFORE ANY EUR/USD TRADE (Vol 7, Ch. 3):
  1. MACRO BIAS: Fed-vs-ECB divergence → long or short bias (Ch. 3)
  2. REGIME: which side of the dollar smile? US–German yield spread confirms? (Ch. 2)
  3. EVENT CHECK: any high-impact US/EZ release imminent? (Ch. 4) → plan around it
  4. SETUP: one of the strategies below, IN the bias direction
  5. RISK: stop at invalidation; size = ≤1% (Vol 1); reward ≥ 2R
  6. EXECUTE & JOURNAL (Vol 4)
```

---

## 6.2 Strategy 1 — Trend Pullback (with macro divergence) ⭐ the core swing setup

EUR/USD's highest-quality trades come when a **Fed-vs-ECB divergence trend** is underway and price pulls back to a quality level. This is the bread-and-butter setup for the pair.

```
WHEN:   EUR/USD is TRENDING (Ch. 5), driven by clear central-bank divergence (Ch. 3).
MARKET: e.g. bias is SHORT (Fed more hawkish than ECB) and the Daily is in a downtrend.

SHORT SETUP (mirror for longs):
  1. Confirm downtrend structure (lower highs/lows) on Daily/H4 (Vol 3, Ch. 3).
  2. Wait for a PULLBACK UP to a quality level: prior resistance / supply zone / a round
     number / the 61.8% golden pocket / a key MA — ideally several stacked (confluence, Vol 3, Ch. 7).
  3. ENTRY: a bearish trigger at that level (rejection candle / bearish engulfing / a
     lower-timeframe CHoCH down — Vol 3, Ch. 2).
  4. STOP: above the level/structure with an ATR buffer (Vol 3, Ch. 6).
  5. TARGET: prior swing low / next support / measured move — require R:R ≥ 2.
```

> 🔑 This setup stacks EUR/USD's best traits: a macro-driven trend, the pair's clean respect for levels, and confluence. A short pullback to resistance *in a Fed-hawkish divergence downtrend* with a rejection candle is the kind of high-conviction trade EUR/USD offers regularly.

## 6.3 Strategy 2 — Range / Mean-Reversion

EUR/USD ranges a lot (Ch. 5), so trading the range is a staple when there's no strong trend.

```
WHEN:   EUR/USD is RANGING — balanced Fed-vs-ECB picture, no fresh catalyst (Ch. 5).
SETUP:
  1. Identify a clean range: horizontal support & resistance tested 2+ times (Vol 3, Ch. 4).
  2. BUY near SUPPORT (with a bullish trigger); SELL near RESISTANCE (bearish trigger).
  3. STOP: just OUTSIDE the range (below support / above resistance) + buffer.
  4. TARGET: the opposite side of the range.
  5. EXIT THE IDEA if price CLOSES decisively outside the range — it may be breaking into a
     trend (Ch. 5); don't fight a genuine breakout.
CAUTION: avoid range trades right before high-impact US/EZ events (Ch. 4) — they break ranges.
```

> 🔑 Range trading EUR/USD works *because* the pair respects levels — but ranges eventually break, usually on a central-bank or data catalyst (Ch. 3–4). Always know what's on the calendar, and respect a decisive breakout rather than fading it into a loss.

## 6.4 Strategy 3 — London Breakout (of the Asian range)

A classic EUR/USD intraday play exploiting its session rhythm (Ch. 5, 7): the Asian session forms a tight range, and the London open often breaks it with momentum.

```
WHEN:   The London session opens (Ch. 7), after a quiet Asian-session consolidation.
SETUP:
  1. Mark the high & low of the Asian-session range (the consolidation).
  2. As London opens and volatility expands, trade a DECISIVE break (candle CLOSE) beyond the
     range — preferably IN the macro-bias direction (Ch. 3) for higher odds.
  3. STOP: back inside the range. TARGET: a measured move / next level / R ≥ 2.
  4. Consider waiting for a RETEST of the broken boundary as new S/R (Vol 3, Ch. 4–5) to filter
     false breaks.
CAUTION: false breaks happen — require a close, and favor breaks aligned with your bias.
```

## 6.5 Strategy 4 — Trading the Post-Data / Post-Central-Bank Move

EUR/USD's biggest catalysts are US/EZ data and the Fed/ECB (Ch. 3–4). Rather than gambling on the spike, trade the **digested directional move** afterward (Vol 6, Ch. 8).

```
WHEN:   After a high-impact release (CPI, NFP, FOMC, ECB, HICP) — once the initial chaos settles.
SETUP:
  1. Do NOT trade the first violent seconds (slippage, whipsaw, wide spreads — Ch. 4).
  2. Let the market digest the SURPRISE (Vol 6, Ch. 4): which way did the divergence shift?
  3. Once a clear direction & structure form, trade a pullback/continuation IN that direction,
     with a normal setup (trigger at a level) and full risk discipline.
  4. The strongest version: the data REINFORCES your existing macro bias → high conviction.
ADVANCED ONLY: holding through the release on a strong thesis (bigger risk — Vol 6, Ch. 8).
```

> 🔑 The amateur tries to trade the CPI/NFP spike and gets chopped up; the pro **waits for the surprise to resolve into a clear trend** and trades *that*, often for the rest of the day or week (Vol 6, Ch. 8). EUR/USD frequently sets a multi-day direction off a major US/EZ release.

## 6.6 Choosing the right strategy for the conditions

Match the setup to EUR/USD's current mode and the calendar:

```
TRENDING (divergence-driven, Ch. 3, 5)   → Trend Pullback (6.2) — the core, highest-conviction play
RANGING (balanced, no catalyst, Ch. 5)   → Range / Mean-Reversion (6.3)
NEW LONDON SESSION after Asian range     → London Breakout (6.4)
AROUND/AFTER major data or CB events     → Post-Data move (6.5); otherwise stand aside into them
```

> 🔑 **No single strategy works in all conditions** (Vol 4). The skill is reading EUR/USD's *mode* (trend vs range, Ch. 5) and the *calendar* (Ch. 4), then deploying the matching setup — always in the macro-bias direction. Master 2–3 of these deeply rather than dabbling in all four (Vol 7, Ch. 3).

## 6.7 A full worked EUR/USD trade

Bringing the whole stack together on one trade:

```
MACRO (Ch. 3):   The Fed has turned notably more hawkish than the ECB (US inflation hot, Fed
                 signaling more hikes; ECB cautious). US–German yield spread widening for USD.
                 → BIAS: SHORT EUR/USD. Daily is in a downtrend.
REGIME (Ch. 2):  Risk-neutral; dollar firm on the right side of the smile. Confirms short bias.
EVENT (Ch. 4):   No high-impact release in the next several hours (checked the calendar).
SETUP (6.2):     On H4, price pulls back UP to a confluence zone: prior support-turned-resistance
                 (polarity) + the 61.8% golden pocket + the round number 1.1000 + the 50 EMA.
TRIGGER (Vol 3): On M15, a bearish engulfing prints at the zone + a CHoCH down. → enter short.
RISK (Vol 1):    Stop above 1.1000/the zone + ATR buffer (say 35 pips). Size so 35 pips = 1%.
TARGET:          Prior swing low ~70+ pips away → R ≈ 2+. Plan: BE at +1R, trail the rest.
MANAGE & JOURNAL (Vol 4): Execute with SL+TP; log the macro reasoning, R, and "followed plan?".
```

Notice: nothing exotic — just EUR/USD's clean technicals + the Fed-vs-ECB macro engine + confluence + tight risk. **That is what mastering EUR/USD looks like in practice.**
