# Chapter 4 — The EUR/USD Economic Calendar

[← The Two Central Banks](03-the-two-central-banks.md) | [Volume 9 Home](README.md) | [Next: Technical Personality →](05-technical-personality.md)

---

EUR/USD has **two economic calendars** — the US side and the Eurozone side — and it reacts to both. That's double the event risk and double the opportunity. This chapter is your specific guide to *which* releases move the pair, *how much*, and *which way* — so you're never blindsided and always know what's coming (Vol 6, Ch. 3–4).

> 🔑 **Checking the EUR/USD calendar daily is non-negotiable** (Vol 6, Ch. 4; Vol 7). Because the pair reacts to both US *and* Eurozone data, you must track both sides. The single most common unforced error is being caught in a position by a high-impact release you didn't know was scheduled.

---

## 4.1 The two-sided calendar

```
            ┌──────────────── EUR/USD ────────────────┐
   US (dollar) side                          EUROZONE (euro) side
   drives FED expectations                   drives ECB expectations
   • FOMC, CPI/PCE, NFP, GDP, ISM            • ECB, HICP, German CPI, PMIs, GDP, Ifo/ZEW
            └──────── the pair reacts to BOTH ─────────┘
```

A EUR/USD trader must watch **both** schedules. A quiet US day can still bring a market-moving Eurozone release, and vice versa.

---

## 4.2 The US side — releases that move EUR/USD

Ranked by typical impact (the dollar half of the pair; Vol 6, Ch. 3; Vol 8, Ch. 2):

| Release | Tier | What a strong US number usually does to EUR/USD |
|---------|------|--------------------------------------------------|
| **FOMC decision + press conference** | 1 | Hawkish Fed → **EUR/USD down** (biggest scheduled mover) |
| **US CPI / Core CPI** | 1 | Hot inflation → hawkish Fed → **EUR/USD down** |
| **PCE (Fed's preferred inflation)** | 1 | Hot → hawkish → **EUR/USD down** |
| **Non-Farm Payrolls + wages** (1st Fri) | 1 | Strong jobs → hawkish → **EUR/USD down** (violent spikes) |
| **US GDP** | 1 | Stronger growth → **EUR/USD down** |
| **ISM PMIs (mfg & services)** | 1 | Strong/expansion → **EUR/USD down** |
| **Fed Chair & official speeches** | 1–2 | Hawkish tone → **EUR/USD down** |
| **Retail sales, jobless claims, JOLTS, PPI, consumer confidence** | 2 | Strong → mildly **EUR/USD down** |

*(Direction shown for a strong/hawkish US surprise; a weak US surprise pushes EUR/USD up. Always judge vs the **consensus** — the surprise moves it, Vol 6, Ch. 4.)*

---

## 4.3 The Eurozone side — releases that move EUR/USD

The euro half (drives ECB expectations; Germany is the anchor economy):

| Release | Tier | What a strong Eurozone number usually does to EUR/USD |
|---------|------|--------------------------------------------------------|
| **ECB decision + press conference** | 1 | Hawkish ECB → **EUR/USD up** |
| **Eurozone HICP (flash inflation)** | 1 | Hot inflation → hawkish ECB → **EUR/USD up** |
| **German CPI** | 1 | Leads/echoes Eurozone HICP → **EUR/USD up** if hot |
| **Eurozone & German flash PMIs** | 1 | Strong/expansion → **EUR/USD up** (leading indicator) |
| **Eurozone & German GDP** | 1 | Stronger growth → **EUR/USD up** |
| **German Ifo & ZEW sentiment** | 2 | More optimism → mildly **EUR/USD up** |
| **ECB officials' speeches** | 1–2 | Hawkish tone → **EUR/USD up** |
| **Eurozone retail sales, German factory orders / industrial production** | 2–3 | Stronger → mildly **EUR/USD up** |

*(Direction shown for a strong/hawkish Eurozone surprise; weak surprises push EUR/USD down. Germany, as the largest member, carries outsized weight — watch German data closely.)*

> 🔑 Note the **mandate asymmetry from Ch. 3** showing up here: the Fed cares about *jobs + inflation*, so US **NFP and CPI** are the dollar-side heavyweights; the ECB is *inflation-focused*, so **Eurozone HICP** is the euro-side heavyweight. These are the releases most likely to move EUR/USD hard from each side.

---

## 4.4 The heavyweight events (circle these)

If you track nothing else, track these — they cause the biggest EUR/USD moves:

```
  ★ FOMC decision & press conference         (US, 8×/year)
  ★ ECB decision & press conference          (EZ, 8×/year)
  ★ US CPI                                    (monthly)
  ★ Eurozone HICP flash inflation             (monthly)
  ★ US Non-Farm Payrolls + wages              (1st Friday monthly)
  ★ US PCE                                     (monthly)
  ★ Eurozone & German flash PMIs              (monthly)
  ★ Fed Chair & ECB President speeches         (between meetings)
```

On these days, expect **sharp volatility, wider spreads, and slippage** (Vol 6, Ch. 8). Plan around them.

---

## 4.5 The monthly rhythm

EUR/USD's data flow follows a rough monthly cadence you can anticipate:

```
EARLY MONTH:   Flash PMIs (late prior month) · ISM PMIs · US NFP (1st Friday) ·
               Eurozone HICP flash · German CPI
MID MONTH:     US CPI & PCE · retail sales · German Ifo/ZEW · Eurozone GDP (quarterly)
THROUGHOUT:    FOMC and ECB meetings (~every 6 weeks, often mid-month) · weekly jobless claims ·
               speeches scattered across the month
QUARTERLY:     GDP releases · the Fed's dot-plot meetings
```

> 🔑 Knowing the rhythm lets you **anticipate the week's catalysts** in your weekly review (Vol 6, Ch. 7; Vol 7, Ch. 5): "NFP Friday, CPI next Wednesday, ECB the following Thursday." You trade *prepared*, not surprised.

---

## 4.6 How to use the calendar for EUR/USD

```
DAILY (pre-market — Vol 7, Ch. 5):
  □ Scan BOTH calendars (US + Eurozone) for today's high-impact releases & their TIMES
  □ Note the FORECAST (consensus) for each — that's what's priced in (Vol 6, Ch. 4)
  □ Flag central-bank meetings/speeches — the biggest movers
  □ Plan AROUND events: which you'll avoid, which you'll trade after

INTERPRETING A RELEASE (Vol 6, Ch. 4):
  1. Actual vs FORECAST → the SURPRISE moves the pair (not the raw number)
  2. Which side? US beat → EUR/USD down; Eurozone beat → EUR/USD up
  3. Policy implication → does it shift the Fed/ECB divergence? (Ch. 2–3)
  4. Check the REACTION → a non-reaction to a beat is itself a tell (Vol 6, Ch. 4)

AROUND HIGH-IMPACT EVENTS (Vol 6, Ch. 8):
  • Default: reduce size / stand aside into the release; trade the digested move after.
  • Expect slippage and wider spreads in the first seconds. Don't chase the spike.
```

> 🔑 The pro doesn't trade the chaos of the release itself — they **trade the clearer trend once the surprise is digested** (Vol 6, Ch. 8). The calendar's job is twofold: keep you from being blindsided, and tell you when the pair's *direction* might be reset by a catalyst.

## 4.7 Chapter summary

```
• EUR/USD reacts to TWO calendars: the US (dollar/Fed) side and the Eurozone (euro/ECB) side.
  Track BOTH daily — being blindsided is the classic unforced error.
• US heavyweights (strong US data → EUR/USD DOWN): FOMC, CPI, PCE, NFP+wages, GDP, ISM, Fed speeches.
• Eurozone heavyweights (strong EZ data → EUR/USD UP): ECB, HICP flash, German CPI, flash PMIs,
  GDP, Ifo/ZEW, ECB speeches. Germany is the anchor — watch German data.
• Mandate asymmetry: US JOBS + CPI move the dollar side hardest; Eurozone HICP moves the euro side hardest.
• Star events: FOMC, ECB, US CPI, EZ HICP, NFP, PCE, flash PMIs, CB-chief speeches — sharp volatility.
• Learn the monthly RHYTHM to anticipate catalysts in your weekly review.
• Interpret via the SURPRISE vs forecast → which side → policy/divergence impact → the reaction.
  Around big events: reduce/stand aside, then trade the digested move (Vol 6, Ch. 8).
```
