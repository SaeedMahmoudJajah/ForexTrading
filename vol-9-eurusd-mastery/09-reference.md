## A. EUR/USD snapshot (Ch. 1)

```
WHAT:       Euro priced in dollars (base EUR / quote USD) — "Fiber"
MATCHUP:    Eurozone (ECB) vs United States (Fed)
STATUS:     Most-traded (~1/5 of FX) & most-liquid pair → TIGHTEST spreads
PIP:        4th decimal; ~$10/pip per standard lot (clean USD pip value)
DXY:        Euro ≈ 58% of DXY → EUR/USD ≈ INVERSE of the dollar index
ROUND #s:   1.0000 (parity — iconic), 1.0500, 1.1000, 1.1500...
PERSONALITY: calm, orderly, range-prone, respects technicals; prime time = London–NY overlap
```

## B. Driver hierarchy (Ch. 2)

```
1. FED vs ECB policy DIVERGENCE          ← the engine
2. US–GERMAN yield differential          ← real-time tell (watch the 2-yr & 10-yr spread)
3. Broad DOLLAR & risk sentiment         ← the dollar smile
4. Relative GROWTH (Eurozone vs US)
5. INFLATION (US CPI/PCE vs Eurozone HICP)
6. EUROZONE-specific risk (periphery/BTP–Bund, energy, politics)
7. POSITIONING & what's PRICED IN

Master question: "What does this mean for the Fed-vs-ECB path, vs what's priced in?"
Direction: Fed more hawkish → EUR/USD DOWN | ECB more hawkish → EUR/USD UP
```

## C. The two central banks (Ch. 3)

```
FED:  dual mandate (inflation + JOBS) · 8 mtgs/yr · fed funds rate · dot plot ·
      moved by US CPI/PCE AND jobs (NFP)
ECB:  primary mandate price stability · 8 mtgs/yr · deposit rate · one policy for 20 economies
      (hawk/dove tension) + fragmentation guard · moved most by Eurozone HICP

THE DIVERGENCE TRADE: whichever bank turns more hawkish vs what's priced in sets the trend.
Watch for PIVOTS (cycle changes) — they drive the biggest EUR/USD trends.
```

## D. Calendar cheat sheet (Ch. 4) — strong-number direction

```
US SIDE (strong US data → EUR/USD DOWN):
  ★ FOMC + presser · US CPI · PCE · NFP+wages · GDP · ISM PMIs · Fed speeches
  (Tier 2: retail sales, jobless claims, JOLTS, PPI, confidence)

EUROZONE SIDE (strong EZ data → EUR/USD UP):
  ★ ECB + presser · Eurozone HICP flash · German CPI · EZ & German flash PMIs · GDP · ECB speeches
  (Tier 2: Ifo/ZEW, EZ retail sales, German factory orders/industrial production)

REMEMBER: the SURPRISE vs forecast moves it; judge which side beat; check the policy/divergence
impact; watch the reaction. Around big events: reduce/stand aside, trade the digested move.
Mandate asymmetry: US JOBS+CPI = dollar-side heavyweights; EZ HICP = euro-side heavyweight.
```

## E. Technical personality (Ch. 5)

```
• Liquid, orderly, RANGE-PRONE, moderate volatility, respects technicals cleanly.
• Volatility is REGIME-DEPENDENT — use ATR for current ranges (don't assume fixed pips).
• Key levels: round numbers / parity, horizontal S/R, prior-period highs/lows, dynamic MAs, ranges.
• Two modes: RANGING (most of the time → mean-reversion) vs TRENDING (divergence-driven → pullbacks).
• Confluence (level + Fib + round number + trigger) works especially well here.
```

## F. Strategy selector (Ch. 6) — always in the macro-bias direction

```
TRENDING (divergence-driven)     → TREND PULLBACK to confluence + trigger  (core, highest conviction)
RANGING (balanced, no catalyst)  → RANGE / MEAN-REVERSION (buy support/sell resistance)
LONDON OPEN after Asian range    → LONDON BREAKOUT (decisive close beyond the Asian range)
AROUND/AFTER major data/CB       → POST-DATA digested move (don't trade the spike); else stand aside

Universal filter: bias → regime → event check → setup → risk (≤1%, R≥2) → execute → journal.
```

## G. Session & time-of-day (Ch. 7) — times approximate GMT (shift with DST)

```
ASIAN  ~00–07:   quiet, ranging → PREP & mark the range (not active trading)
LONDON ~07–16:   volatility expands (euro's home) → active window
OVERLAP ~12–16:  ★ PRIME ★ peak liquidity, tightest spreads, biggest moves, US data lands
NY PM  ~16–21:   thins out; late NY/rollover = avoid

WEEK: Mon quiet · TUE–THU most active (most catalysts) · Fri = NFP (1st wk) + position-squaring ·
      weekend = GAP risk.
BEST: London + the OVERLAP, Tue–Thu, and the digested post-data move.
AVOID: dead Asian hours, the rollover, the first seconds of data, late Friday.
```

## I. Daily prep checklist (Ch. 8)

```

  □ State check (calm/rested)         □ Update scorecard → today's bias
  □ Both calendars (US+EZ) + times    □ US–German yield spread & DXY check
  □ Mark key levels (round #s, S/R)   □ Mark Asian range high/low
  □ Plan: bias, setups, max trades, max loss
DURING (London + overlap):
  □ With-bias A+ setups only          □ Full chain checked  □ SL+TP, ≤1% risk
  □ Stand aside into big data; trade the digested move  □ Stop at target/daily limit
POST:
  □ Journal every trade (+ "did I follow my plan?")  □ Update scorecard  □ One lesson
```
