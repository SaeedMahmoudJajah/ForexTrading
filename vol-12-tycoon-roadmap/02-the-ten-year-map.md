# Chapter 2 — The 10-Year Map

---

A decade is too long to navigate without a map. This chapter gives you the whole territory at once: the five phases, what each is *for*, the gates between them, and the compounding math that makes the back half of the decade so much more powerful than the front. Read this as the bird's-eye view; the chapters that follow zoom into each phase. The single most important idea here is **sequence** — doing the right thing in the wrong year is how people stall or blow up.

> 🔑 **The order is the strategy.** Skill before size, size before scale, scale before enterprise. Almost every retail failure is a *sequencing* error: risking big before having an edge, going full-time before being consistent, managing others' money before mastering their own. The map's job is to keep you doing the right thing *for the year you're actually in*.

---

## 2.1 The five phases

The decade breaks into five phases, each with a single dominant job:

```
PHASE 0  PREP        (Months 0–6)   Decide, learn the basics, set up. Risk: ~$0 (demo).
PHASE 1  APPRENTICE  (Years 1–2)    Build skill & survive. Small live capital. Keep income.
PHASE 2  PROFESSIONAL(Years 2–4)    Consistency → funded capital → full-time.
PHASE 3  ALLOCATOR   (Years 4–7)    Scale capital, diversify income, run it as a business.
PHASE 4  ENTERPRISE  (Years 7–10)   Trade others' capital, build a firm/team, multiply yourself.
```

Each phase has a **gate** — a standard you must meet before advancing. Gates are the safety mechanism of the whole plan: they stop you from scaling a mistake.

| Phase | Dominant job | Gate to the next phase |
|-------|--------------|------------------------|
| 0 Prep | Learn & set up | A written trading plan + a strategy to test (Vol 7) |
| 1 Apprentice | Skill & survival | ~6+ months of journaled, rule-following, break-even-or-better demo→micro trading |
| 2 Professional | Consistency & funding | A real, documented edge + funded capital + a cash buffer to go full-time (Vol 5) |
| 3 Allocator | Scale & diversify | Stable full-time income from trading + diversified savings + a business structure |
| 4 Enterprise | Multiply yourself | A repeatable system others can run + capital base to scale |

> ⚠️ **Never skip a gate, no matter how good a year feels.** A hot streak in Phase 1 is not permission to risk Phase 3 size — it's usually variance that will revert and punish the over-sizer. The gates exist precisely because *feeling* ready and *being* ready are different. Meet the documented standard, then advance. Discipline at the gates is what survival looks like at the scale of years.

## 2.2 The compounding engine — why patience pays

The reason the plan is ten years and not two is **compounding**, and the reason the early years feel slow is that compounding is back-loaded. A simple illustration (purely to show the *shape*, not a promise):

```
ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY — assumes a modest, consistent annual return on growing capital,
with capital also rising as you add funds and win allocations. Real results vary wildly.

  Year 1   ▏ small                  ← feels like nothing is happening
  Year 2   ▎
  Year 3   ▍
  Year 4   ▋                        ← skill + capital both compounding now
  Year 5   █▏
  Year 6   █▋
  Year 7   ██▌                      ← the curve visibly steepens
  Year 8   ███▊
  Year 9   █████▍
  Year 10  ███████▋                 ← most of the wealth is created HERE

  The bars are small for years, then explode. That back-loaded shape is WHY
  most people quit (in the flat early years) — and why the patient win.
```

> 🔑 **The flat early years are not wasted — they are the foundation the steep years stand on.** Compounding looks like failure right up until it looks like magic. The trader who quits in year 2 because "it's not working" is quitting *the year before it starts to work*. Trust the curve, do the work in the flat part, and let time do what only time can.

## 2.3 The three things that compound

It's not just money that compounds across the decade — three distinct things grow on top of each other, and that *stacking* is the real engine:

```
1. SKILL compounds      — each year of journaled experience (Vol 4, 10) makes your
                          edge sharper and your mistakes rarer. Year 8 you ≫ Year 1 you.
2. CAPITAL compounds     — reinvested profits + added savings + funded allocations +
                          (later) others' capital. More money behind the same edge.
3. REPUTATION/BUSINESS   — a track record opens funded accounts, then investors, then
   compounds              partnerships and a firm. Trust accumulates like interest.

   SKILL × CAPITAL × REPUTATION, all rising together = the tycoon's exponential.
```

A beginner has none of these. The tycoon has spent a decade stacking all three. You can't shortcut the stack — but you *can* make sure every year adds to all three rather than just grinding one.

## 2.5 A one-page mental model to carry for ten years

If you forget everything else, keep this:

```
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  EVERY YEAR, ASK:                                                       │
│   • Did my SKILL improve? (better KPIs, fewer mistakes — Vol 4)         │
│   • Did my CAPITAL grow? (profits + savings + allocations)             │
│   • Did my BUSINESS/REPUTATION advance? (track record, structure)      │
│   • Did I SURVIVE? (no blow-up, within drawdown limits)                │
│                                                                         │
│  If yes to all four, you are on the tycoon track — regardless of how    │
│  big this year's number was. Stack them for ten years. That's the plan. │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

> 🔑 **Judge each year by the four questions, not by the bank balance.** Early on, the balance barely moves while skill and reputation build invisibly — and that's *success*, because those are what the later capital compounds on. Measure the inputs that create wealth, trust the lagging output, and you'll keep doing the right thing long enough for the decade to pay.
