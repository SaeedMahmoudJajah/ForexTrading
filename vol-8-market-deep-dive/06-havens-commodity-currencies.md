# Chapter 6 — Havens & Commodity Currencies

---

This chapter profiles four currencies that sit at the two ends of the risk spectrum: the **Swiss franc** (a pure safe haven) and the three **commodity dollars** — the **Australian dollar, New Zealand dollar, and Canadian dollar** (risk-on, growth-and-commodity currencies). Together they show the cleanest examples of how *role* drives behavior (Vol 8, Ch. 1).

---

## 6.1 The Swiss Franc (CHF) — the European safe haven

```
ROLE         Safe haven (alongside USD & JPY)
NICKNAME     "Swissie"
CENTRAL BANK Swiss National Bank (SNB) — known for intervention
PERSONALITY  Stable, haven-bid in fear; shadows the euro; SNB can move it abruptly
SESSION      European (London) session
```

- **Why it's a haven:** Switzerland's political neutrality, stability, sound finances, and strong external position make the franc a refuge in times of fear — it **strengthens in risk-off** (Vol 6, Ch. 5).
- **It shadows the euro:** Switzerland's economy is tightly linked to the Eurozone, so **CHF broadly tracks the euro**, and **USD/CHF is close to a mirror image of EUR/USD** (Vol 8, Ch. 3).
- **SNB intervention is legendary:** the SNB actively manages franc strength (excessive strength hurts Swiss exporters). The most famous event: in **January 2015 the SNB abruptly removed its EUR/CHF floor**, and the franc spiked ~20–30% in *minutes* — a brutal lesson in **central-bank and gap risk** (Vol 6, Ch. 5).

> 🔑 The franc behaves like a "European yen" — a haven that strengthens in fear but is tethered to the euro and subject to **abrupt SNB action.** Respect SNB intervention risk; the 2015 floor removal remains the cautionary tale of how a central bank can detonate a "stable" currency overnight.

## 6.2 The Commodity Dollars — an overview

The Australian, New Zealand, and Canadian dollars are **commodity currencies / "commodity dollars"** — their economies export raw materials, so their currencies rise and fall with commodity prices and global growth. They're also **risk-on currencies**: they strengthen when the world is optimistic and sell off in risk-off (the opposite of the havens).

```
RISK-ON  → commodity dollars (AUD, NZD, CAD) STRENGTHEN (growth/yield seeking)
RISK-OFF → commodity dollars WEAKEN (risk dumped, money flees to havens)
```

> 🔑 The commodity dollars are the **risk-on counterparts to the havens.** Pairing a commodity dollar against a haven (e.g. AUD/JPY) creates a pure risk-sentiment barometer (Vol 8, Ch. 7). Know each one's specific commodity and growth linkage.

---

## 6.3 The Australian Dollar (AUD) — "Aussie"

```
ROLE         Commodity dollar + risk-on barometer + China proxy
NICKNAME     "Aussie"
CENTRAL BANK Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA)
SESSION      Asian (Sydney/Tokyo) session
```

- **Drivers:** prices of Australia's key exports — **iron ore, coal, metals** (and gold) — plus **Chinese growth** (China is Australia's biggest export market), global **risk sentiment**, and the **RBA** rate path.
- **The China connection:** AUD is widely used as a **liquid proxy for Chinese growth and global risk appetite** (Vol 6, Ch. 6). Strong Chinese data / commodity demand → AUD up.
- **Personality:** a classic **risk-on currency** — AUD/USD tends to rise when stocks rise and commodities are strong, and fall in risk-off.

> 🔑 To trade the Aussie, watch **metals prices, Chinese economic data, and the global risk regime** alongside the RBA. It's one of the purest "global growth optimism" plays in FX.

---

## 6.4 The New Zealand Dollar (NZD) — "Kiwi"

```
ROLE         Commodity dollar + risk-on currency (smaller, higher-beta cousin of AUD)
NICKNAME     "Kiwi"
CENTRAL BANK Reserve Bank of New Zealand (RBNZ)
SESSION      Asian session
```

- **Drivers:** **agricultural / dairy** exports (New Zealand is a major dairy exporter), global **risk sentiment**, the **RBNZ** path, and — by association — **Chinese/Asian demand**.
- **Tightly correlated with AUD:** the Kiwi and Aussie move closely together (similar regions, both commodity/risk currencies). NZD is often the slightly **higher-beta, less-liquid** of the two.

> 🔑 NZD and AUD are close cousins — trading them simultaneously is often nearly the *same* bet (correlation risk, Vol 1). The **AUD/NZD cross** isolates the specific Australia-vs-New-Zealand divergence when you want it.

## 6.5 The Canadian Dollar (CAD) — "Loonie"

```
ROLE         Commodity dollar — the OIL currency
NICKNAME     "Loonie" (after the loon bird on the C$1 coin)
CENTRAL BANK Bank of Canada (BoC)
SESSION      New York session (overlaps US hours; tied to the US economy)
```

- **The oil link:** Canada is a major **oil exporter**, so the Loonie is strongly tied to **crude oil prices**. **Oil up → CAD up → USD/CAD down** (and vice versa). This is one of the clearest commodity-currency relationships (Vol 6, Ch. 6).
- **The US connection:** the US is Canada's dominant trading partner, so CAD is sensitive to **US economic health** and moves partly with US data — USD/CAD is a bit of a "North American" pair.
- **Driver mix:** oil prices + the **BoC** rate path + US economy/data + risk sentiment.

> 🔑 If you trade USD/CAD, **watch crude oil as a key input** — it often leads the Loonie. Note the tension in USD/CAD: a strong US economy lifts the dollar (USD/CAD up) but can also support oil/risk (CAD up) — so the pair is a tug-of-war between the dollar's drivers and oil's.

---

## 6.6 Comparison & how to use these profiles

| Currency | Role | Key drivers | Watch |
|----------|------|-------------|-------|
| **CHF** 🇨🇭 | Safe haven | Risk-off flows, euro link, SNB | Risk regime, EUR, SNB |
| **AUD** 🇦🇺 | Commodity / risk | Metals, China, risk, RBA | Iron ore, China data, stocks |
| **NZD** 🇳🇿 | Commodity / risk | Dairy, risk, RBNZ (tracks AUD) | Dairy, risk, AUD |
| **CAD** 🇨🇦 | Commodity (oil) | Oil, US economy, BoC | Crude oil, US data |

> 🔑 These four show *role* in action: **CHF strengthens in fear** (haven); **AUD, NZD, CAD strengthen in optimism** (risk/commodity). Combine each with its specific commodity/growth linkage and central bank to build its macro scorecard (Vol 6, Ch. 7). And mind correlations — AUD/NZD are near-twins; commodity dollars often move together in risk-on/off.

## 6.7 Chapter summary

```
• CHF ("Swissie") = European SAFE HAVEN: strengthens in risk-off, shadows the euro (USD/CHF ≈
  inverse EUR/USD), and is subject to abrupt SNB INTERVENTION (the 2015 floor removal: ~20-30%
  in minutes — gap/central-bank risk).
• AUD, NZD, CAD = COMMODITY DOLLARS / risk-on currencies: strengthen in optimism, weaken in risk-off.
• AUD ("Aussie"): metals + CHINA proxy + risk + RBA. NZD ("Kiwi"): dairy + risk + RBNZ, tracks AUD.
• CAD ("Loonie"): the OIL currency (oil up → CAD up → USD/CAD down) + US economy + BoC.
• Use ROLE to predict risk-on/off behavior, then layer each currency's specific commodity/growth
  driver and central bank. Mind correlations (AUD≈NZD; commodity dollars move together).
```
