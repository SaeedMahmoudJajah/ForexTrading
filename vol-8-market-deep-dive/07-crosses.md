So far we've focused on **majors** — pairs that include the US dollar. **Cross pairs (or "crosses")** are pairs that *don't* include the USD: EUR/GBP, EUR/JPY, GBP/JPY, AUD/JPY, and more. They let you trade the relationship between two non-dollar currencies directly — isolating a view without the dollar's noise. This chapter explains how crosses behave and profiles the most important ones.

---

## 7.1 What a cross is — and how it's "built"

A **cross** is any pair without the USD. Mechanically, a cross is derived from two dollar pairs:

```
EUR/JPY  ≈  EUR/USD  ×  USD/JPY        (the dollar "cancels out")
GBP/JPY  ≈  GBP/USD  ×  USD/JPY
EUR/GBP  ≈  EUR/USD  ÷  GBP/USD
```

This has two consequences:
1. **A cross reflects the *relative* strength of its two currencies**, stripping out the dollar — useful when you have a view on, say, "the euro vs the pound" specifically.
2. **Crosses inherit the traits of both legs** and can be **more volatile** (two currencies' moves combine) and often have **slightly wider spreads / less liquidity** than the majors.

> 🔑 Use a cross when your view is about **two specific non-dollar currencies** (e.g. ECB vs BoJ), and you want to remove the dollar from the equation. If your view is really a *dollar* view, trade the major instead. Match the instrument to the view.

---

## 7.2 The risk-sentiment crosses ⭐ (commodity dollar vs yen)

The most useful crosses for reading the market are **commodity-dollar-vs-yen** pairs — they pair a **risk-on currency against a safe haven**, making them **pure risk-sentiment barometers** (Vol 8, Ch. 1):

### AUD/JPY — the classic risk barometer
```
ROLE: risk-on currency (AUD) vs safe haven (JPY) → the cleanest RISK GAUGE in FX
  • RISK-ON  → AUD up + JPY down → AUD/JPY RISES (often hard)
  • RISK-OFF → AUD down + JPY up → AUD/JPY FALLS (often hard)
```
AUD/JPY is so reliable as a risk gauge that traders watch it as a **sentiment indicator** even when they don't trade it — it often moves with global equities (Vol 6, Ch. 6). NZD/JPY behaves similarly.

> 🔑 **AUD/JPY is the market's risk thermometer.** When it's ripping up, the world is risk-on; when it's collapsing, risk-off is here. Use it as a confirming read on the risk regime (Vol 6, Ch. 5) regardless of what you trade.

---

## 7.3 GBP/JPY — "the Beast" / "the Dragon"

```
ROLE         High-volatility risk + yield-differential cross
NICKNAME     "the Beast," "the Dragon," "Guppy"
PERSONALITY  One of the MOST VOLATILE commonly-traded pairs — huge ranges, fast moves
```

- Combines the **pound's volatility** (Vol 8, Ch. 4) with the **yen's risk/carry sensitivity** (Vol 8, Ch. 5) — the two amplify each other.
- Driven by **risk sentiment**, the **BoE–BoJ rate differential**, and **carry dynamics** (it's a higher-yielder vs the funding yen).
- ⚠️ **Notorious for large, fast swings** — exhilarating and dangerous. It demands **wide stops, small size**, and serious respect.

> 🔑 GBP/JPY earned its "Beast" nickname honestly. Its huge ranges offer big opportunity but punish over-sizing brutally. **Only trade it with volatility-appropriate stops and reduced size** (Vol 1, 3) — it's an advanced pair, not a beginner's playground.

---

## 7.4 EUR/JPY — the steadier risk cross

```
ROLE         Risk-sentiment + ECB/BoJ differential cross
PERSONALITY  Liquid, smoother than GBP/JPY but still risk-sensitive
```

- A widely-traded, relatively liquid cross combining **risk sentiment** with the **ECB–BoJ rate differential.**
- Acts as a **risk barometer** (like AUD/JPY) but is generally **calmer than GBP/JPY** — a middle ground: risk-sensitive without the Beast's wildness.

---

## 7.5 EUR/GBP — the range-bound European cross

```
ROLE         Two correlated European economies (Eurozone vs UK)
PERSONALITY  Often RANGE-BOUND, lower volatility; driven by ECB-vs-BoE & relative data
SESSION      London session (both home markets)
```

- Pairs two **closely linked, geographically-adjacent economies**, so the pair tends to be **range-bound and less volatile** — it moves on the *relative* policy and data of the **ECB vs the BoE** and Eurozone-vs-UK performance.
- Isolates the **pound-vs-euro** story directly (without the dollar). Useful around UK vs Eurozone divergences (e.g. BoE hiking while the ECB holds).
- Its **range-prone personality** suits range/mean-reversion strategies (Vol 3, Ch. 5) — but watch for political catalysts (UK events, Vol 8, Ch. 4) that can break the range.

> 🔑 EUR/GBP is the calm, range-trading European cross — the instrument for expressing a "UK vs Eurozone" view cleanly. Because the two economies are intertwined, big trends are rarer; it spends much of its time oscillating in ranges.

---

## 7.6 Other crosses & the general principles

Other crosses (EUR/CHF, GBP/AUD, AUD/NZD, EUR/AUD, etc.) follow the same logic — combine the two profiles:
- **EUR/CHF:** two linked European economies + a haven → historically very stable (and the site of the 2015 SNB shock — Vol 8, Ch. 6).
- **AUD/NZD:** two near-twin commodity dollars → low-volatility, isolates Australia-vs-NZ divergence.
- **GBP/AUD, EUR/AUD:** combine a European major with a commodity dollar → can be volatile, blends risk-sentiment and rate stories.

**General principles for trading any cross:**
```
□ READ BOTH legs' profiles and combine them (risk-vs-haven? two similar economies?).
□ Expect MORE VOLATILITY and slightly WIDER SPREADS than the majors (size accordingly).
□ Trade during the SESSION when both currencies are active for best liquidity.
□ Use a cross to express a NON-DOLLAR view; if it's really a dollar view, use the major.
□ Watch CORRELATIONS — don't unknowingly stack the same risk (e.g. multiple yen crosses
  in risk-off is one big "short yen-crosses" bet — Vol 1).
```

## 7.7 Chapter summary

```
• CROSSES = pairs without the USD; they're "built" from two dollar pairs and reflect the
  RELATIVE strength of two non-dollar currencies. Often more volatile, slightly wider spreads.
• Use a cross for a NON-DOLLAR view; use the major if it's really a dollar view.
• RISK-SENTIMENT CROSSES (commodity dollar vs yen): AUD/JPY (& NZD/JPY) are the market's
  cleanest RISK BAROMETERS — up in risk-on, down in risk-off. Watch AUD/JPY as a sentiment read.
• GBP/JPY ("the Beast"): pound volatility + yen risk/carry = one of the WILDEST pairs — wide
  stops, small size, advanced only. EUR/JPY: similar but calmer.
• EUR/GBP: two linked European economies → RANGE-BOUND, lower-vol; expresses UK-vs-Eurozone.
• For any cross: combine both legs' profiles, size for the volatility, trade the right session,
  and mind correlation stacking.
```
