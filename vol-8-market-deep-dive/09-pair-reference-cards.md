One quick-reference card per major instrument — nickname, central bank, drivers, personality, best session, correlations, and key data. Skim before a session; use to choose and study your focus pairs (Vol 7, Ch. 3). All behaviors are *tendencies*, re-checked against the current regime (Vol 6).

## Currency roles at a glance

```
RESERVE/CENTER:  USD              SAFE HAVENS:      USD, JPY, CHF (+ gold)
EUROPEAN MAJORS: EUR, GBP         COMMODITY DOLLARS: AUD, NZD, CAD
RISK-ON  → commodity dollars up, havens down   |   RISK-OFF → havens up, commodity dollars down
```

---

## EUR/USD — "Fiber"
```
Bank: ECB (EUR) vs Fed (USD)      Session: London / London–NY overlap
Personality: most liquid, tightest spreads, calm, orderly, range-prone — the home base
Drivers: Fed-vs-ECB divergence · broad dollar (DXY) · Eurozone/German data · periphery
         (fragmentation) risk · energy
Correlations: strongly INVERSE DXY & USD/CHF · positive with GBP/USD · tracks EZ–US yield gap
Key data: ECB & Fed decisions · Eurozone/German CPI, GDP, PMIs · US CPI, NFP, GDP
Trade as: mostly a DOLLAR trade + the ECB side. Lean on its clean technicals.
```

## GBP/USD — "Cable"
```
Bank: BoE (GBP) vs Fed (USD)      Session: London / London–NY overlap
Personality: VOLATILE — bigger/faster ranges than EUR/USD, headline-sensitive
Drivers: BoE path (watch MPC vote split) · broad dollar · UK data (CPI, wages, GDP) ·
         risk sentiment (semi risk-on) · UK POLITICS (elevated headline risk)
Correlations: positive with EUR/USD (higher-beta) · inverse DXY · EUR/GBP = pound-vs-euro
Key data: BoE decisions · UK CPI, jobs/wages, GDP, PMIs · UK budgets/elections · US data
Trade as: dollar trade + UK story. WIDER stops, SMALLER size. Mind political headlines.
```

## USD/JPY — "Ninja" / dollar-yen
```
Bank: Fed (USD) vs BoJ (JPY)      Session: Tokyo / London–NY overlap
Personality: tracks US YIELDS closely; trends in calm, reverses VIOLENTLY in risk-off
Drivers: US–Japan yield differential (esp. US 10-yr) · risk sentiment (yen = haven) ·
         BoJ policy · intervention risk at weak-yen extremes
Correlations: STRONG positive with US 10-yr yield · positive with equities (risk-on) ·
              yen crosses = amplified versions
Key data: BoJ & Fed decisions · US yields/Treasuries · Japanese CPI · risk gauges (VIX)
Trade as: macro pair — trade WITH the US-yield trend; respect risk-off yen spikes & intervention.
```

## USD/CHF — "Swissie"
```
Bank: Fed (USD) vs SNB (CHF)      Session: London (European)
Personality: stable; CHF = safe haven that shadows the euro; SNB can move it abruptly
Drivers: risk sentiment (CHF haven, strengthens in risk-off) · euro link · SNB intervention ·
         the dollar
Correlations: near-MIRROR of EUR/USD (USD/CHF inverse to EUR/USD) · CHF tracks EUR
Key data: SNB & Fed decisions · Swiss CPI · risk regime · (watch SNB intervention signals)
Trade as: haven + euro-shadow + dollar. Respect SNB/gap risk (the 2015 floor-removal lesson).
```

## AUD/USD — "Aussie"
```
Bank: RBA (AUD) vs Fed (USD)      Session: Asian (Sydney/Tokyo)
Personality: risk-ON barometer + China proxy; rises in optimism, falls in risk-off
Drivers: metals (iron ore, gold) · CHINESE growth/data · global risk sentiment · RBA · the dollar
Correlations: positive with risk/equities, metals, NZD · inverse DXY
Key data: RBA decisions · Australian CPI/jobs · Chinese data · commodity prices · US data
Trade as: a "global growth optimism" play — watch metals, China, and the risk regime.
```

## NZD/USD — "Kiwi"
```
Bank: RBNZ (NZD) vs Fed (USD)     Session: Asian
Personality: commodity/risk currency; close cousin of AUD (higher-beta, less liquid)
Drivers: dairy/agri exports · risk sentiment · RBNZ · Asian/Chinese demand · the dollar
Correlations: STRONG positive with AUD (often the same bet) · risk-on assets · inverse DXY
Key data: RBNZ decisions · NZ CPI/jobs/dairy · risk gauges · US data
Trade as: a risk-on commodity play; AUD/NZD isolates Australia-vs-NZ divergence.
```

## USD/CAD — "Loonie"
```
Bank: BoC (CAD) vs Fed (USD)      Session: New York (overlaps US hours)
Personality: the OIL currency; a "North American" pair tied to the US economy
Drivers: CRUDE OIL (oil up → CAD up → USD/CAD down) · US economy/data · BoC · risk sentiment
Correlations: USD/CAD INVERSE to oil · sensitive to US data
Key data: BoC & Fed decisions · Canadian jobs/CPI · crude oil · US data
Trade as: watch CRUDE OIL as a key input; note the dollar-vs-oil tug-of-war within the pair.
```

## Key CROSSES

```
AUD/JPY — risk-ON currency vs haven → the market's cleanest RISK BAROMETER (up=risk-on,
          down=risk-off). NZD/JPY similar. Watch as a sentiment read even if not trading it.
GBP/JPY — "the Beast/Dragon": pound volatility + yen risk/carry = one of the WILDEST pairs.
          Wide stops, small size, advanced only.
EUR/JPY — risk-sensitive ECB/BoJ cross; calmer than GBP/JPY.
EUR/GBP — two linked European economies → RANGE-BOUND, low-vol; expresses UK-vs-Eurozone.
AUD/NZD — near-twin commodity dollars → low-vol; isolates Australia-vs-NZ.
GENERAL: a cross = both legs' profiles combined; more volatile & wider spreads than majors;
         use for NON-dollar views; mind correlation-stacking (e.g. many yen crosses = one bet).
```

## GOLD & EXOTICS

```
XAU/USD (Gold) — safe haven / "anti-dollar." Drivers: REAL YIELDS (down → gold up), the dollar
   (usually inverse), fear, inflation, central-bank buying. Watch as a barometer from day one.
   (Inverse-to-USD is a tendency; both can rise in extreme panic.)
EXOTICS (USD/MXN, USD/ZAR, USD/TRY, USD/CNH...) — major + EM currency. High yield BUT high
   volatility, wide spreads, GAP/TAIL & political risk ("up the stairs, down the elevator").
   ADVANCED ONLY — mostly observe while building skill on the majors.
```

## How to use these cards (Vol 7, Ch. 3)

```
1. CHOOSE 1–3 focus pairs whose personality fits your style, schedule & risk tolerance.
   (Beginners: start with EUR/USD — liquid, cheap, orderly.)
2. STUDY their cards deeply; build each currency's macro scorecard (Vol 6).
3. WATCH the right things: each pair's key data, correlated markets, and active session.
4. SIZE for personality: wider stops + smaller size on volatile pairs (GBP/JPY, Cable).
5. AVOID correlation-stacking: don't unknowingly take the same bet across correlated pairs.
6. RE-CHECK vs the current regime — these are tendencies, not laws.
```

> 🔑 Don't trade all of these — **master the few you choose.** Depth in 1–3 pairs (their drivers, rhythm, correlations, and tells) beats shallow familiarity with all of them (Vol 6, Ch. 7; Vol 7, Ch. 3).
